Review and Lock Project Costs

Overview

Review your project job costs in the Details tab of each cost centre. You can manually edit the sub-total or total, or you can lock the job to prevent changes. You can also lock individual cost centres within the job.

Review project job costs

Go to Details > Summary in the cost centre of a project job to view the estimated and actual costs of Parts & Labour.

The costs listed in the Actual column are compared with the costs in the Estimate column. Actual figures that indicate an increase in profit will appear in green, while figures that show a decrease in profit will appear in red.

Before reviewing job costs, click Save to ensure the following items are up to date:

Materials Cost The total cost of all billable materials added to the cost centre.

Resources Cost

The total costs associated with labour and plant resources added to the cost centre, including overhead. Click the grey drop-down arrow to view the following costs:

  • Labour is the total costs associated with labour resources, excluding the overhead.
  • Labour Hours is the total number of labour hours added to the job.
  • Overhead is the total overhead recovery that applies to the job based on the number of applicable resource hours.

Materials Markup

 The overall markup on materials in absolute value. This figure represents the difference between the total materials cost and the total materials sell.

Resources Markup

 The overall markup on resources in absolute value. This figure represents the difference between total resource cost and total resource sell.
Adjusted The value subtracted from the Sub Total to account for any changes made the cost centre after it has been locked.
Discount / Fee

The discount or fee added as an absolute value or as a percentage of the combined Materials Cost, Resources Cost, Materials Markup, and Resources Markup.

To add a discount / fee, click the pencil icon, then select Down / Up, enter either a percentage or an absolute value, select if the discount / fee is Tax Inclusive, then click Save.
Sub Total The combined Materials Cost, Resources Cost, Materials Markup, Resources Markup and Discount / Fee. Click the pencil icon to manually adjust this price.
Tax code applied The tax applied to the Sub Total. Click the pencil icon to select a different tax code, then click Save.
Total The combined Sub Total plus tax. Click the pencil icon to manually adjust this price.
Gross P / L The combined Overhead, Materials Markup, and Resources Markup.
Gross Margin The Gross P / L divided by the Sub Total as a percentage.
Nett P / L The combined Materials Markup and Resources Markup.
Nett Margin The Nett P / L divided by the Sub Total as a percentage.
Invoiced Value The absolute value that has already been invoiced for the job.
Invoice Percentage The percentage of job costs that have already been invoiced.

Adjust the project job sell price

To adjust the Sub Total / Total for a cost centre in a project job:

  1. Access the desired job, then go to Cost Centre List and click the desired cost centre.
  2. Go to Details > Summary > Parts & Labour.
  3. Click the pencil icon next to the Sub Total / Total.
  4. Enter the desired Cost Centre Price.
    • The Sub Total must be tax exclusive, while the Total must be tax inclusive.
  5. Select a different Tax Code if desired.
  6. Click Save.

When you adjust the Sub Total / Total, the pencil icon will turn green.

Lock a project job

You can lock multiple cost centres or the entire job from the Cost Centre List, or you can lock the entire job from the Project Overview.

Locking a job using either of these two methods will lock the job price, as well as either the budget or items as defined in System > Setup > Defaults > System > Jobs > Lock Jobs. You may wish to check these settings before locking a job to ensure you are locking it correctly.

Lock a project from the Cost Centre List

To lock a job from the Cost Centre List:

  1. Access the desired project job, then click Cost Centre List.
  2. Select the check boxes for each cost centre you would like to lock.
    • Alternatively, select Select All Visible.
  3. Click Lock in the footer options.
  4. Click OK > Save / Finish.

Lock a project from the Project Overview

To lock the job from the Project Overview, access the job as normal, then go to Project Overview > Adjustments, and click Lock Job > OK.

Lock a cost centre

To lock an individual cost centre, you can Lock Estimates & Price or Lock Items & Price.

To lock a cost centre:

  1. Access the desired job, then go to Cost Centre List.
  2. Click the desired cost centre.
  3. In the Details tab, click:
    • Lock Estimates & Price to lock the sub-total and total but still allow changes to individual items and labour.
    • Lock Items & Price to lock the cost centre and prevent changes to individual items and labour.

If you Lock Estimates & Price, the Revised column will appear next to the Estimate column. Any changes you make will display here.

Lock the revised budget

You may wish to lock the revised budget before starting the project so that the total does not change continually while the job is in progress.

To lock the revised budget:

  1. Access the desired job, then go to the Cost Centre List.
  2. Click the desired cost centre, then go to Details > Summary > Parts & Labour.
  3. Click Lock Revised Budget.

Once the revised budget is locked, you won't be able to adjust the labour or any of the items or you have already added. You can still make items non-billable, edit pre-builds and add new items as necessary, but this will not affect your totals.

To unlock the revised budget, click Unlock Revised Budget.

Items added after the revised budget has been locked will automatically be made non-billable. If you want to make them billable, it is recommended to unlock the revised budget first.
