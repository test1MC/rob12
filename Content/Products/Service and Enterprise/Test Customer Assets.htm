Test Customer Assets (Maintenance Planner only)

Overview

With Maintenance Planner, technicians can test customer assets on the job and record the results, which you can then see in simPRO. Test results can be recorded in a job card, or in Connect. If an asset fails a test you can immediately create a quote for fixing it.

Record test results in a job card

After a customer asset has been tested on a maintenance job, you can manually record the test results in a job card.

To record test results:

  1. Access the relevant job card.
  2. Click Assets.
  3. If necessary, select the relevant Service Level for the asset.
  4. Click Pass / Fail to pass or fail the asset.
    • If the asset has failed, select the relevant failure points and recommendations.
  5. Click Approve.

If you have accessed the job card from the Schedules tab in the job, you will have the option to Quote Asset Failures when you exit the job. Click this to view failed assets and create quotes to rectify them.

Record test results in Connect

If your technicians use our Connect mobile app, they can test assets as part of the job workflow. You can then view the recorded results in the job card.

 View failed assets

If an asset has failed a test, you will receive an alert in simPRO.

To view failed assets:

  1. Go to the Alerts page:
    • From the dashboard, click Jobs in the Alerts box.
    • Alternatively, click the red alerts icon in the System menu in the upper right.
  2. Click the relevant asset failure alert in the Alert column under Jobs.

Failure points for the asset will be listed in the table, as well as any recommendations selected by the technician. To create a quote for the work to fix the failed asset, click Options > Create a quote. If you want to automatically add any pre-builds associated with the failure points to the quote, make sure that Combine pre-builds on quote is selected when you create the quote.

To defer the alert for a certain period of time, enter the number of months at the bottom of the table, select the desired alert, then click Do not prompt again for # months. To permanently remove the alert without creating a quote, click Options > Do not prompt again.
