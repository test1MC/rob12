Raise a Customer Credit Note

Overview

Raise customer credit notes in simPRO to record credited and voided customer invoices. There are two types of credit notes you can create in simPRO:

Credit Note

This is considered a sales returned for accounting purposes and offers customers a discount off the original invoice raised against a job. You can raise a credit note for a partial or full amount of the invoice, but you cannot unlock and modify the job once the credit note is raised.

Void Invoice

This option voids the full amount of the invoice if, for example, the invoice has been raised incorrectly. When the invoice has been voided, the invoice status will be set to Fully-paid to prevent any payments being applied, and the relevant job will be moved from Invoiced to Complete. You can then access and modify the job as desired and create a new invoice.

It is recommended you consult your accountant on the best practice for crediting and voiding invoices for your business.

Raise a customer credit note

To create a customer credit note:

  1. Go to Invoices > Unpaid / Paid Invoices.
  2. Locate the desired invoice, then click Options > Create Credit Note.
  3. If you are crediting a paid invoice, click the invoice, then click Payments > Add Credit Note.
  4. Select whether your want the Invoice Values to include or exclude tax.
  5. Select whether your want the Invoice Values to include or exclude tax.
  6. Select Credit Note as the Credit Note Type.
  7. Enter the Date Issued and Order No. as applicable.
  8. Select the Cost Centre Account. To use the same cost centre as the invoice, select Original Invoice.
  9. Enter a Credit Note Comment if desired.
  10. Review and update the Credit Amount and Retention if desired.
  11. Review and update the Credit Amount and Retention if desired.
  12. Review and update the Credit Amount if desired.
  13. Click Finish.
 Void an invoice

To void an invoice:

  1. Go to Invoices > Unpaid / Paid Invoices.
  2. Locate the desired invoice, then click Options > Create Credit Note.
  3. If you are voiding a paid invoice, click the invoice, then click Payments > Add Credit Note.
  4. Select whether your want the Invoice Values to include or exclude tax.
  5. Select whether your want the Invoice Values to include or exclude tax.
  6. Select Void Invoice as the Credit Note Type.
  7. Enter the Date Issued and Order No. as applicable.
  8. Select the Cost Centre Account. To use the same cost centre as the invoice, select Original Invoice.
  9. Enter a Void Invoice Comment if desired.
  10. Review the Void Amount and Retention.
  11. Review the Void Amount and Retention.
  12. Review the Void Amount.
  13. This will be restricted to the full invoice amount.
  14. Click Finish.
 Email a credit note

To email a credit note:

  1. Go to Invoices > Credits, then click the desired credit note.
  2. Alternatively, raise a credit note / void an invoice as normal, then click Finish.
  3. Click the Forms tab.
  4. Under Form, ensure Credit Note is selected.
  5. Alternatively, select Email Only to send an email with no form.
  6. Click View ​to preview the form.
  7. Select Show as Copy to add a Copy watermark to the form if desired.
  8. Select the Primary Contact to address the email to, then click to add additional Contacts as desired.
  9. Alternatively, you can manually enter email addresses and separate them with a comma.
  10. Adjust the default Subject and Body if desired. The default is set up in System > Setup > Forms Setup > Credit > Email.
  11. Alternatively, click Clear Email to enter a new Subject and Body.
  12. Click Send > OK to confirm.

Go to System > Setup > Forms Setup > Credit Note > Display Options > Form Title to enter a new form title to display on the printable form document. The form will still retain its simPRO default title when you select it from the Form drop-down list.
