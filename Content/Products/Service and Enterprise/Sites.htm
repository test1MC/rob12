Sites

Overview

In simPRO, sites are physical locations where you perform work for a customer.

You can identify a site by a defined site name that your business will easily recognise. This may be the same as the street address, or alternatively, a building name or customer name, for example, 'ABC Lighting - Head Office'.

Sites must be associated to customers in order to be selected when creating a job or quote. You may wish to associate multiple sites to one customer, or in some cases, multiple customers to one site.

View sites

To view your list of sites, hover over People in the main menu, then click Sites in the Go To sub-menu.

To locate a site, click the starting letter of the site name above the search bar. Alternatively, enter the site name, ID number, address or associated customer in the search bar and click Search.

If you have a large site database, you can narrow your search results by Site Status, Account Manager, State and Zone by clicking Advanced.

You can then save these filter options as a saved search to access a site or group of sites more quickly.

Access the following options when you right-click or click Options on a site in the Sites table:

Enter the site card file and edit details. You can also access the site card file by clicking the site in the Sites table.

Merge Site

Merge a site and all of its details, jobs and quotes into another selected site. This is recommended if you have duplicated sites in your system.

Archive Site

Archive a site. You can view archived sites by going to People > Sites > Advanced > Site Status, then selecting Archived or Active & Archived. To make a site active in your system again and allow quotes and jobs to be created against them, click Options > Activate Site.

Delete Site

Permanently delete a site.

You can map, merge, archive and delete multiple sites at once by selecting the site check boxes, then clicking the desired option below the table.

 Create a site

To create a site in simPRO, hover over People, then click Create New Site. Alternatively, you can create a new site within a customer, quote or job. You can then manage site details, enter custom information, add site contacts, add a preferred technician and adjust tax settings.

To create a site:

  1. Hover over People, then click Create New Site.
  2. Alternatively, go to People > Sites > Create Site.
  3. Enter a Site Name that clearly identifies the site.
  4. Search and select a Zone if applicable.
  5. Enter Street Address details.
  6. (AU) Enter the Suburb and select the State to automatically generate the Postcode.
  7. (UK) Enter the Postcode, then click outside the text box to generate a selection of addresses.
  8. Enter the Postcode, then click outside the text box to generate a selection of addresses.
  9. Enter the Suburb and select the State to automatically generate the Postcode.
  10. Enter Primary Contact details for the site.
  11. Click Save or Finish.

Postal Contact and Postal Address information is required only if a billing address differs from a site address and customer billing address. For example, if your customer is a real estate company, the Site Address will be a tenant's physical address, and you will use the Postal Contact and Postal Address if you need to enter an owner's name for billing and override the billing address saved in the customer's card file.

If you are conducting solar industry work in Australia and have enabled STC or VEEC zones, you can also select a zone from the STC / VEEC Zone drop-down list.

 Manage customer assets

Create and manage customer assets in the Customer Assets tab of a site to provide preventative maintenance and repair.

You can create customer assets based on asset types, custom fields and test readings defined in System > Setup > System Setup > Customer Assets > Asset Builder.

To create a customer asset:

  1. Access the desired site.
  2. Click Customer Assets > Create Customer Asset.
  3. Select the Customer Asset Type.
  4. Select the associated customer Contract if applicable.
  5. Enter Custom Fields information as required.
  6. Click Attachments to upload asset documents or photographs.
  7. Click Related Assets.
  8. Search and select an existing Parent Asset if applicable.
  9. Click Finish.
Manage customer asset service levels (Maintenance Planner Only)

You can manage and add service levels to track the service and testing frequency for an asset. Service levels, as well as failure points and recommendations, need to be created in System > Setup > System Setup > Customer Assets > Service Levels before being assigned to an asset.

To add service levels:

  1. Access the desired site, then go to Customer Assets.
  2. Create or access the asset as normal, then go to Service Levels.
  3. Under Add a service level, select the desired service level from the drop-down list, then click Add.
  4. Enter the applicable Service Date for the asset. When using Maintenance Planner, this will be the next service date to create a job for the asset.
  5. Click Save or Finish.
  6. Click the red delete icon to remove a service level.

Edit Customer Asset

Enter the customer asset and edit details. You can also access the asset by clicking it.

Create Job

Print Barcode

Maintenance Planner

Delete Customer Asset

Associate customers with a site

Use the Customers tab to create or add existing customers to associate with a site. If you have created a site within a customer card file, the customer will automatically be associated.

To create a new customer to associate to a site:

  1. Access the desired site, then go to the Customers tab.
  2. Click Create Customer.
  3. Enter details as required, then click Finish.

To add an existing customer to a site, search and select the Customer Name, then click Add.

To remove a customer from a site, click Options > Remove from Site, or alternatively, select the customer, then click Remove from Site.

As customer assets are managed within sites, multiple customers associated to one site may be able to access other customer assets when accessing jobs and quotes in the customer portal.

 View site history

Use the History tab in a site card file to view a history all quotes, jobs and invoices raised against the site in simPRO.

 Manage site attachments

Add, organise and download site-specific attachments, such as blueprints and maps, in the Attachments tab of a site card file.

 Enter site notes

Enter site-specific notes in the Notes tab of a site card file. Public Notes are visible in Connect and could include public site information such as parking instructions. Private Notes are for internal reference only and are not visible in Connect.
