Quote Job Detail Form

Overview

With this editable form you can provide a detailed PDF document to customers for a quote or job, as might be needed for large projects and tenders. You may also choose to use it for smaller quotes and jobs. You can create detail forms from scratch each time, but it is more efficient to create and use templates.

After opening an existing quote or job, the Quote Detail Form or Job Detail Form is available on the Forms tab, under the Form drop-down list.

Unlike other forms in the list, clicking this form will open a pop-up window. From this window you can edit the form and send it to your customer along with your quote or job, then return to the open quote or job.

Create a detail form template

Creating and using detail form templates makes your detail forms efficient and consistent. When you use a detail form template to generate your detail form, you can edit it to suit that particular quote, job, or customer.

To create a detail form template:

  1. In an existing quote or job, click the Forms tab.
  2. In the Forms drop-down list, select Quote Detail Form or Job Detail Form (depending on which you are viewing). A Quote / Job Detail Form pop-up window will open.
  3. In the Detail tab, click Manage.
  4. Click Create Template.
  5. Enter the name of your template, then click Save. The Edit Template page will open.
  6. Use the Block drop-down list to add blocks and rearrange them as desired.
  7. Click Finish, then OK. Your new template will ready to use from the Template drop-down list.

Add blocks

Use blocks to structure your quote or job detail form and customise the information displayed.

To add a block to a detail form or detail form template:

  1. Navigate to the Edit Template or Project Detail page within your quote or job detail form.
  2. Select the desired block from the Block drop-down list and click Add.
  3. Your new block will appear at the bottom of the page.
  4. Use the drag handles to rearrange the blocks and any associated fields.

To edit a block in detail, click Manage Items.

To delete unwanted fields or an entire block, click the associated Delete icon.

Block types

Acceptance Page

Usually found towards the end of a form, this can include a cost structure summary, acceptance message, space for a customer signature, and purchase order details.

  

Cost Centre Item List

A list of cost centre items.

  

Custom

A customisable block.

  

Employee Contact Detail

Usually found at the bottom of quotes and jobs, this can include employee and company details, along with an employee signature image file (if saved to the employee's card).

  

How To Pay (End Of Page)

Insert your How To Pay information (as entered in your Forms Setup) at the bottom of the current page.

  

New Page

A page break to control your document's layout and readability.

  

Optional Cost Centre Item Lists

A list of optional cost centre items.

  

Overview Description

The text entered into the Description field in the quote or job's overview.

  

Project Total

Project total and sub-total details.

  

Project Total (End Of Page)

Project total and sub-total details, at the bottom of the current page.

  

Summary

A summary of costs broken down by cost centre.

  

To edit a Custom block:

  1. Add a Custom block to your detail form.
  2. On the new block, click Manage Items.
  3. Click Create Item.
  4. Enter information and format as desired.
  5. Click Save.
  6. Repeat as needed, then click Close.
  7. On the custom block, use the right drop-down list to select whether your item appears as text, paragraph text, a bulleted list, or a numeric list.

Edit a detail form template

To edit an existing detail form template:

  1. In an existing quote or job, click the Forms tab.
  2. In the Forms drop-down list, select Quote Detail Form or Job Detail Form (depending on which you are viewing).
  3. In the Detail tab, click Manage.
  4. Click the template you wish to edit.
  5. Edit as desired, then click Finish and OK.

If you want to edit a detail form for a specific quote, job or customer, do not edit the template as these changes will be permanent. Instead, add or delete individual items after selecting the detail form template.

Delete a detail form template

To delete an existing detail form template:

  1. In an existing quote or job, click the Forms tab.
  2. In the Forms drop-down list, select Quote Detail Form or Job Detail Form (depending on which you are viewing).
  3. In the Detail tab, click Manage.
  4. Click Options on the template you wish to delete.
  5. Click Delete and OK.

Use a detail form template

To use a detail form template to email a customer a quote or job detail form:

  1. Open the quote or job and finalise any changes.
  2. In the Forms tab, select Quote Detail Form or Job Detail Form (depending on which you are viewing).
  3. In the Detail tab, select the desired detail form template from the Template drop-down list.
  4. Enter or edit the Title, Revision No., and Introduction as needed. The Title will be the name of your PDF.
  5. Edit the form blocks as desired to control the information this specific customer sees.
  6. If you wish to add attachments, upload on the Attachments tab.
  7. Click the Forms tab.
  8. From the Form drop-down list, select the type of details you want to display (such as Itemised w/Prices).
  9. Edit the remaining email fields as needed.
  10. Click Send and OK.
  11. Click OK again, then click Finish to exit the detail form window.

Before emailing, use the Attach button on the Forms tab to keep a copy of this form in your system for future reference.

Advanced

Page break for optional cost centres

If the System > Setup > Forms Setup > Quote > Sections > Options > Section Always Start In New Page option is enabled, your optional cost centres will display on a new page.

When using the quote detail form, this setting will be replaced with your quote detail form setup structure. Make sure you have included the Optional Cost Centre Item Lists block.

Adjust the quote detail form title

Go to System > Setup > Forms Setup > Quote > Display Options > Form Titles to enter a new form title to display on the printable form document. The form will still retain its simPRO default title when you select it from the Form drop-down list.

You can also enter a new Estimate Form Title to display when you select Show as Estimate when emailing.
