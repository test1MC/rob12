Create a Pre-Build

Overview

Pre-builds combine materials and labour as a single billable item to appear on jobs, quotes and invoices. The two main types are standard price pre-builds and set price pre-builds, and you can adapt these as desired. You can use pre-builds for many functions, including:

  • Maintenance Planner
  • Maintenance Planner
  • assemblies
  • schedules of rates
  • renewable energy
  • contractors.

Pre-builds can also be a useful feature for your customers who use the customer portal. You can set up your portal so that if customers request a quote or book a job through the portal, they can select the work they need done from a list of pre-builds (such as 'Install double powerpoint').

Before creating pre-builds you will need to Create a Pre-Build Group.

Create a standard price pre-build

Create a standard price pre-build if you want your pre-build's sell price to accurately reflect all costs involved, including materials, labour and associated markup.

A pre-build's Pricing Type cannot be changed after the pre-build is first used.

To create a standard price pre-build:

  1. Go to Materials > Pre-Builds.
  2. Create or open the group or subgroup you want your new pre-build to go in.
  3. Click Create Pre-Build.
  4. Enter the pre-build Name.
  5. If required, enter a Part No. and select a Sales Tax Code. See below for important notes about pre-build tax codes.
  6. Under Pricing Type, select Standard Price.
  7. Under Material:
    1. Select whether the pre-build's Cost price is Based on Build, or manually enter the Cost.
    2. Select whether the pre-build's Sell markup is Based on Defaults, Based on Build, or manually enter a percentage or Sell price.
  8. Under Labour:
    1. Select whether the pre-build's Time is Based on Defaults, or manually enter the Minutes.
    2. Select whether the pre-build's Rates are Based on Defaults, or manually enter the labour rate information.
  9. If desired, enter Notes.
  10. Click the Catalogue tab to continue.

Sales Tax Code: If you are working in countries with differing tax codes, such as the US, you may wish to select certain tax codes on your pre-build:

  • For materials-only pre-builds, select Use default part tax code or a specific materials tax code
  • For labour-only pre-builds, select Use default labour tax code or a specific labour tax code
  • If your pre-build has both materials and labour, select any tax code but ensure you check and update line item tax codes in each quote or job as required.

Notes about material and labour prices:

  • If the Material Cost is Based on Build, the Material Cost in the Break Down will be the total cost of items in the Build tab. This allows catalogue price updates to reflect in the pre-build.
  • If the Material Sell markup is Based on Build, pricing tiers will be applied to individual items in the Build tab to determine the pre-build's markup and sell value.
  • If the Material Sell price is Based on Defaults, the pre-build's material markup will be based on system defaults. If the pre-build is then used in a quote or job where the customer has a different material markup, that markup will reflect in the pre-build.
  • If the Labour Time is Based on Defaults, the pre-build's time will be a total of estimated fit time associated with the individual items added to the Build tab.
  • If the Labour Rates are Based on Defaults, the pre-build's labour will be charged according your system's default labour rates. If the pre-build is then used in a quote or job where the customer has different labour rates, those rates will reflect in the pre-build.

Add catalogue items to your pre-build through the Catalogue tab

To add catalogue items to your pre-build through the Catalogue tab:

  1. Use the Search bar to find the desired item, or open the relevant groups and subgroups.
  2. Locate the desired item, then enter the number required and click Add Items.
  3. Repeat as necessary.
  4. Click the Build tab to continue.

Confirm prices and build time through the Build tab

You can view and edit items added through the Catalogue tab on the Build tab.

To confirm items and build time through the Build tab:

  1. Review and edit the Qty for each time as required. To remove an item, click the red delete icon.
  2. If you selected the Cost or Sell prices to be Based on Build in the Details tab, check the prices in this tab are accurate.
  3. Check the Time (Mins) is accurate. To edit or add time for an item, click the item, enter an Estimated Time, then click Finish to close the pop-up window.
  4. Select a Fit Time from the drop-down list to suit your pre-build as required.
  5. To continue, go to the STCs tab or Attachments tab, or click Finish.
  6. To continue, go to the STCs tab or Attachments tab, or click Finish.
  7. Go to the Attachments tab to add any attachments.
  8. Click Finish.
Optional: STCs and Attachments tabs

If STCs are enabled in your system, use the STCs tab to turn them On or Off for this pre-build, and fill out details in your STCs List and / or VEECs List as required.

For more information about STCs, read our article about Solar Industry Set-up.

If you would like to add attachments to your pre-build, such as a product image, use the Attachments tab.

When you have adjusted your pre-build as required, click Finish.

 Create a set price pre-build

Create a set price pre-build if you want a pre-determined sell price for your pre-build, regardless of the costs involved. These pre-builds are commonly used for creating a schedule of rates, and are necessary for maintenance-related pre-builds if you are using simPRO Enterprise with Maintenance Planner purchased and enabled.

A pre-build's Pricing Type cannot be changed after the pre-build is first used.

To create a set price pre-build:

  1. Go to Materials > Pre-Builds.
  2. Create or open the group or subgroup you want your new pre-build to go in.
  3. Click Create Pre-Build.
  4. Enter the pre-build Name.
  5. If required, enter a Part No. and select a Sales Tax Code. Read important notes about pre-build tax codes.
  6. Under Pricing Type, select Set Price.
  7. Select whether you want the Time to be Based on Build, or enter the number of Minutes the pre-build will take to complete.
  8. Enter the Price you want this pre-build to cost.
  9. If desired, enter Notes.
  10. Click the Catalogue tab to continue.

 Edit a pre-build

To edit a pre-build:

  1. Go to Materials > Pre-Builds.
  2. Locate and open the desired pre-build.
  3. Make changes as desired.
  4. Click Finish.

If you need to edit a pre-build with history, it is recommended to make a copy of it first by clicking Options > Copy Pre-Build, and edit the copy as needed. Alternatively, if you need to make a once-off change for a specific quote or job, add the pre-build to the quote or job then edit it from there.

 Archive or delete a pre-build

When you archive or delete a pre-build, it will still appear in any existing jobs or quotes that it has been added to, but will not be available to add to new jobs and quotes. If a catalogue item that has been included in a pre-build is deleted, it will be removed from the pre-build.

To archive or delete a pre-build:

  1. Go to Materials > Pre-Builds.
  2. On the desired pre-build, click Options > Archive / Delete Pre-build.
  3. Click OK to confirm.

To archive or delete multiple pre-builds at once:

  1. Go to Materials > Pre-Builds.
  2. Select the check boxes of the pre-builds you wish to delete, or click Select All.
  3. At the bottom of the page, click Archive or Delete.
  4. Click OK to confirm.
