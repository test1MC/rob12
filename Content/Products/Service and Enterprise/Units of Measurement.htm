Units of Measurement

Overview

Use units of measurement to manage quantities of items.

For example, you may have an item that you buy from your suppliers in bulk, but sell to your customers in smaller increments. You can then use units of measurement to distinguish whether certain items can be sold as fractions of the whole item or not.

Create a unit of measurement

To create a unit of measurement:

  1. Go to System > System Setup > Materials > Units of Measurement.
  2. Click Create Unit of Measurement.
  3. Enter the Unit of Measurement Name.
  4. If applicable, select Restrict to whole numbers to prevent this item from being allocated in fractions of a unit, for example, 1.5 units.
  5. Click Create.

To delete a unit of measurement, click the red delete icon. To delete multiple units of measurement, select the check boxes on the left and click Delete.

If you sell different items in the same unit of measurement, but one can only be used in whole quantities, while another can use fractions of whole quantities, you need to create two different units of measurement; one that is restricted to whole quantities, and one that is not.

 Apply a unit of measurement to catalogue items

To apply a unit of measurement to a catalogue item:

  1. Go to Materials > Catalogue.
  2. Click the desired item.
  3. Select the applicable Unit of Measurement in the Details tab.
 Units of measurement in quotes and jobs

When adding items to jobs, items that are restricted to whole quantities will not allow quantities with fractions of a unit to be added, for example, 1.5 units.

If you enter a quantity for an item that is not restricted to whole numbers, and the quantity has more than two decimal places, it will automatically round to two decimal places.
