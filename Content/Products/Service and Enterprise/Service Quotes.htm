Service Quotes

Overview

Create quotes for 'do and charge' jobs, or service quotes, in simPRO. When the quote has been approved, you can then convert it to a service job. You can also archive service quotes if they are unsuccessful.

Technicians using our Connect mobile app can also create service quotes in the field.

Simplified service quote workflow

Below is the simplified workflow for a service quote. Click any step to view the corresponding instructions below.

 Create a service quote

You can create a service quote from the Quotes menu, from the schedule Day View / Week View, or from within the customer / site card file.

To create a service quote from the Quotes menu:

  1. Go to Quotes > Create New Quote / Create New Service Quote.
  2. Search and select a Customer and Site from the drop-down lists.
    • Alternatively, click Create New to create a new customer and / or site.
  3. Select a Customer Contact and Site Contact, or click Create New to create new contacts.
    • The contact phone numbers will then be available in Connect. The Site Contact may be a store manager, building manager etc.
  4. Select the desired Cost Centre.
    • Cost centres separate operational costs and services within your business to assist in reporting.
  5. Enter the quote Description to appear on the quote card, in Connect, and in the converted job if the quote is successful.
    • Alternatively, select a script from the Insert Script drop-down list. You can then adjust the description as desired using the text formatting options.
  6. Assign a Salesperson and / or Project Manager and enter a Due Date if desired.
    • You need to mark employees as Salesperson / Project Manager in their card files to select them.
  7. Assign a Salesperson and enter a Due Date if desired.
    • You need to mark employees as Salesperson in their card files to select them.
  8. Assign a Salesperson and / or Project Manager and enter a Due Date if desired.
    • You need to mark employees as Salesperson / Project Manager in their card files to select them.
  9. Select Tags to categorise the quote.
  10. Click Finish to create and exit the quote.
    • Alternatively, click Next to continue building the quote.

When you have clicked Finish on a service quote, a Quote Submitted pop-up will appear where you can choose to:

View Quotes View all quotes in the Open / ProgressQuotes table, depending on how you have set up your automatic triggers in System > Setup > System Setup > Status Codes > Automatic Triggers.
Convert To Convert the quote to a Job, Recurring Job, or Recurring Invoice.
Edit Quote Go to the Summary tab to continue building the quote.
Schedule Quote Schedule a technician to a quote in the Day View / Week View, depending on your settings in System > Setup > Defaults > Schedule > Default View.

You can return to the Quote Setup page to make changes by accessing the desired quote, then going to Details > Settings > Quote Setup.

 View the quote summary

Go to Details > Summary in a service quote to adjust the quote description, enter private notes visible in Connect but not visible to the customer, Review Estimated Costs and Margins, and view a timeline of all quote activity in simPRO.

In the remaining Details sub-tabs, you can adjust quote settings, email a quote form to the customer, upload quote attachments and view a log of the quote history. In simPRO Enterprise, you can also Create service quote tasks.

Within each sub-tab, click the black drop-down arrow in the top right to view customer and site details, the quote total, the assigned technician if applicable and the cost centre description.

Adjust quote settings

Go to Details > Settings in a service quote to adjust the overall settings for the quote. In addition to being able to Enable retention / retainage and enter information in custom fields set up in System > Setup > System Setup > Custom Fields > Leads, Quotes, Jobs & Recurring, you can also access the following options:

SMS Quote If you have registered for and set up SMS in simPRO, you can send SMS to mobile devices directly from the quote.
Copy Quote Create an identical copy of the service quote with a different quote number and creation date.
Quote Setup Return to the Quote Setup to change the customer and / or site. This is the first page you access when you Create a service quote.

You can also enter a Request No. supplied by the customer to add additional reference information to the quote, as well as an Order No. if required by the customer.

Adjust the date the quote was Created if desired, as well as the Due Date and Validity period for the quote to receive system alerts. You can also assign a Salesperson and Project Manager if the relevant employees have been designated in their employee card files.

To manually move the quote to a different Stage or change the Status, select as applicable from the drop-down list. Alternatively, select Auto Adjust to use automatic triggers.

Quotes will appear twice in Connect if you both assign and schedule the applicable Technician.

 Manage parts and labour in a service quote

Go to Parts & Labour > Billable to manage all billable and non-billable parts and labour in a service quote. You can Add catalogue items, pre-builds and take off templates (take off templates are only available in simPRO Enterprise), Create and add one off items, and Manage labour in a service quote. In simPRO Enterprise, you can link a supplier quote by selecting a Supplier and Supplier Quote, then clicking Apply.

In the Parts table, you can adjust the estimated Time for an item, and, in simPRO Enterprise, select a Fit Time multiplier. When adjusting Time for items, remember this will affect the Estimated Time and Time Billed in the Labour table.

In addition to the Time, you can adjust the Price, Markup, Sell Price and Qty of items. To reset the Price, select the check box or click Options > Reset Pricing. Clicking Options will also allow you to open and edit an item, remove it, change the tax code or make it non-billable.

You can also go to Parts & Labour > Stock to view the current available stock items you may wish to include in your quote. Adding stock items at the quote level does not affect your stock levels.

Add catalogue items, pre-builds and take off templates

To add catalogue items, pre-builds and, in simPRO Enterprise, take off templates to a service quote:

  1. Access the desired quote.
  2. Go to Parts & Labour > Catalogue / Pre-Builds / Take Off.
  3. Locate the desired items in their applicable Groups and / or Subgroups.
  4. Within each group / subgroup, enter the desired quantity per item, then click Add Items.
    • Alternatively, click Create Pre-Build / Create Item to create a new pre-build / catalogue item.
  5. Click Billable.

  6. Manage parts and labour in a service quote as normal.

    • ​​If you are adding a pre-build, click Options > Edit Pre-Build On Quote to adjust the materials in the pre-build only in that quote. The original pre-build quantity will not be affected.
  7. Click Save or Finish.
 Create and add one off items

Create one off items to assign non-catalogue expenses that will not be processed through simPRO, for example, parking, government charges, and custom-engineered tools.

To create a one off item in a service quote:

  1. Access the desired quote, then go to Parts & Labour > One Off Items.
  2. Click Add One Off Item.
  3. Select the item Type.
  4. Select Billable to add the item to the customer invoice.
  5. Select Inventory Item to order the item and track in the Stock tab of the converted job.
  6. Enter a detailed item Description.
  7. Enter the Estimated Cost and Markup % / Sell Price.
    • If the item has associated labour, enter an Estimated Time in minutes.
  8. Enter the Quantity of items required.
  9. Select a Sales Tax Code.
  10. Click Finish.
  11. Click Billable.
  12. Manage parts and labour in a service quote as normal.
  13. Click Save or Finish.
 Manage labour in a service quote

A labour rate that has been marked as default in the customer card file will be automatically selected in the Labour table when the quote is created. To add a labour rate, select as desired from the Labour drop-down list, then click Add. You can then adjust the Markup, Sell Price and Time as desired.

You can also adjust labour automatically to be based on hours added when you Schedule a service quote from the Schedule tab. Click Options on a labour rate to remove it or change the tax code if desired.

To add labour rates to a quote, you need to assign them in the customer card file. If the default labour type for the quote has been automatically selected and the check box is selected, any estimated times associated with catalogue items or pre-builds will automatically add to the billable hours.

 Email a quote form to the customer

Email quote forms to the customer using the form templates assigned in System > Setup > Forms Setup > Quote > Forms. You can also select and customise a Quote Detail Form to send to the customer.

To email a quote form to a customer:

  1. Access the desired quote, then go to Details > Forms.
  2. Select the desired Form depending on the amount of detail you wish to provide the customer.
    • To send an email with no form, select Email Only.
    • Select Show as Estimate to label the form as an 'estimate' rather than a 'quotation' if desired.
  3. Click View to preview and / or print the form.
  4. Select a Primary Contact to address the form to and click to add additional Contacts if desired.
    • Alternatively, you can manually enter email addresses and separate them with a comma.
  5. Leave the Subject and Message fields blank to send the email template as set up in your forms setup.
    • Alternatively, enter a Subject and Message to override it.
  6. Click Send > OK to confirm.

A pop-up will then appear confirming the email address you have just sent the form to. Click OK to return to the quote, then click Attach to attach a copy of the sent form to the quote for your records.

 Schedule a service quote

There are several ways to schedule service quotes to specific technicians in simPRO, for example, if you need to perform an onsite visit to complete the quote:

  • In the Schedule tab of a service quote.
  • In the schedule Day View / Week View / Manual View.

Before you begin scheduling service quotes to resources such as employees, and in simPRO Enterprise, contractors and teams, you may wish to use a number of simPRO scheduling tools to increase scheduling accuracy and efficiency.

Schedule a service quote from the Schedule tab

Go to Schedule in a created service quote to schedule technicians, and, in simPRO Enterprise, teams to the quote.

To schedule a quote in the Schedule tab:

  1. Access the desired quote, then go to Schedule.
  2. Under Available Resources, click the calendar icon next to the employee, or in simPRO Enterprise, the contractor or team you wish to schedule.
    • Click an employee / contractor / team to view their available hours over the next five days.
  3. In the top left of the schedule view, select the desired schedule rate.
  4. Click the desired time block to add a schedule block.
    • Use the white drag icon to move the schedule block to a different day or hour.
    • Use the white arrows to lengthen or shorten the schedule block.
  5. When you have finished scheduling, click the close icon in the top right.
  6. Click Save.

Alternatively, click Help me decide to schedule a quote using Filter Options, including the duration of the quote, the Zone, and Travel Distance to the site.

 Advanced Create a service quote with multiple cost centres

You can create service quotes with multiple cost centres if the converted job would require works performed by different areas in your business. This process depends on whether you have single cost centre quotes enabled in your system default settings in System > Setup > Defaults > System > General > Service Jobs and Quotes.

You can also add optional cost centres to a quote to offer customers a choice of options or additional options as an upselling technique. See Managing Optional Quotes for more information.

To create a service quote with multiple cost centres when you do not have single cost centre quotes enabled:

  1. Create a service quote as normal.
  2. Click Next.
  3. In Cost Centre List > Primary, select the desired Cost Centres from the drop-down box, then click Add.
    • ​​Once you have built your quote you may wish to use the Stage, Budget Status and Name / ID filter options to narrow your selection.
  4. Click Finish to create the quote.

Click Options on a cost centre to duplicate, copy, edit, delete, or mark it as optional / billable.

 Create service quote tasks

Create tasks within service quotes to keep an interactive to-do list for the quote. Then when a task is due or has been updated, the assigned technician(s) as well as any other nominated stakeholders, such as customers or employers, will receive email notifications and simPRO alerts.

You can also add task templates created in your System > Setup > System Setup > Tasks > Task Templates to add sets of tasks frequently carried out for certain types of quotes.

 Enable retention / retainage

Retention, also known as retainage in the United States, is the percentage of payment held by the customer to ensure the job is completed to specific standards and to safeguard against defects in workmanship.

You may wish to enable retention / retainage for a service quote that you plan to merge into a project job. This is performed using the same process as for project quotes and is useful for calculating the amounts to be withheld to assist in invoicing.

Learn how to Enable retention / retainage
