Project Quotes

Overview

Create project quotes for multi-stage projects by accurately estimating your material and labour costs and effectively mark up your sell prices to stay competitive.

You can then send it to the customer for approval before you Convert a Quote.

Simplified project quote workflow

Click the steps in the workflow below to view the relevant instructions below.

 Create a project quote

To create a project quote:

  1. Go to Quotes > Create New Project Quote.
  2. Search and select a Customer, or click Create New to create a new one.
  3. Search and select a Site.
  4. Select a Customer Contact. This is the person who will receive the quote.
  5. Select a Salesperson.
  6. Select Tags to categorise the quote.
  7. Click Optional to check or adjust the default rates that will be applied to the quote.
  8. Click Next.
  9. Go to Details > Info and enter a Description of the project. This will appear on the quote.
    • You can also enter information under Notes. This will not appear on the quote and is for your own reference.

You can then continue building the quote by adding sections and cost centres.

Add sections and cost centres

The main difference between a project quote and a service quote is that a project quote is split into sections. You can create sections to define different stages of the job, such as Fit Off, Rough In and Commissioning, and then add cost centres to the each section so you can add parts and labour.

To add sections:

  1. Create or access the project quote as normal.
  2. Click Cost Centre List.
  3. Enter the section Name. This will appear on the quote.
  4. Enter a Description if desired. This will not appear on the quote and is just for your reference.
  5. Click Add.

Continue adding sections as desired. To add cost centres to sections:

  1. In the Cost Centre List, locate the desired section.
  2. Select the check boxes for each Cost Centre you wish to add.
  3. Click Add.

Continue adding cost centres to each section as desired.

You can also add a Description for each cost centre in Details > Info to appear on the quote. When you have set up your sections and cost centres, you can then add parts and labour.

 Add optional cost centres

If you are using the quote to present a customer with options that they can chose from, add optional cost centres in the Options sub-tab. You can choose to make all your cost centres optional if you are presenting your customer with a selection of potential works, or you can have optional cost centres in addition to your primary cost centres if you are upselling your customer something in addition to the requested work.

See Managing Optional Quotes for more information.

 Add parts and labour

In a project quote, add parts and labour to each cost centre within each section. This helps you build your material and labour per section in your quote, and will enable you to later manage your costs against your estimates once the quote becomes a project.

To add parts and labour to a project quote:

  1. Access the project quote as normal, then go to Cost Centre List.
  2. Click a cost centre.
  3. Click Parts and Labour.
  4. Click the sub-tabs to add Pre-Builds, Catalogue items, and One Off Items as desired.
  5. Click Billable.
  6. Select one or more labour rates and click Add.
  7. Adjust the estimated Time if needed.
  8. Click Save.

To return to the Cost Centre List to continue adding parts and labour to each cost centre in each section, click Cost Centres in the breadcrumbs at the top of the project quote.

You can also add Take Off Templates to a project quote. You can only add a take-off template to one section, so if the take-off template encompasses a large amount of work, it may be best to add the individual components to each section where they are needed.

 Adjust material or labour markup

Go to Project Overview to view a summary of the project that includes the details of each cost centre.

Go to Project Overview > Materials to view the total cost of materials and adjust the sell price or markup if desired.

To adjust the sell price or markup for all cost centres, enter the adjustment in the Adjust Material Sell / Adjust Material Markup field near the top of the page, then click Update > Save.

Go to Project Overview > Resources to adjust total labour hours and labour markup if desired.

You can change this for individual cost centres, or for all cost centres. To adjust the labour hours or labour markup for an individual cost centre, enter the adjustment in the correct column and click Save.

To make adjustments to the entire project, enter the Adjust Material Markup % and Adjust Labour Markup %. Click Update Quote to see the affect on your margins in the graph and summary, then click Save. Other adjustments you can make against the entire project from this tab include Material Adjust Sell $, Global Adjust Hours and Will Adjust Hours by %.

Go to Project Overview > Adjustments to adjust the materials and labour in the same way as Project Overview > Materials / Resources, as well as apply a fee or discount to the entire quote.

To apply a fee or discount:

  1. Under Adjustment type, select Down (Discount) or Up (Fee).
  2. Enter the adjustment as a Value or Percentage.
  3. Click Update Quote.

The fee / discount will display as a separate colour in the chart on the right.

 Add customer assets

If the project will involve servicing customer assets on site, you can add these in the Customer Assets tab in the relevant cost centre.

To add an asset to the project quote:

  1. Access the desired project quote.
  2. Go to Cost Centre List, then click the applicable cost centre.
  3. Click Customer Assets.
  4. Under Add Asset, search and select the desired asset.
  5. Click Save.
 Add more customers

If you are creating a tender to send to several people, you can add additional customers as recipients. Add more customers in the Additional Customers tab and you will be able to select them in the Forms tab.

To add more customers to a project quote:

  1. In the project quote, go to Details > Additional Customers.
  2. Search and select a customer or click Create New to create a new one.
  3. Select the relevant Site.
  4. Optionally, select a Contact, Additional Contacts and Site Contract.
  5. Click Save.

Repeat the steps to add customers as necessary.

 Email a project quote

Once you have finished creating the project quote, email it to the customer.

To email a quote:

  1. Access the desired project quote as normal, then go to Details > Forms.
    • If you are inside a cost centre, click Project Quote # in the breadcrumbs at the top of the project quote.
  2. If you have multiple customers, select the desired Customer. This will automatically fill in the email address from their customer card.
  3. Select the type of Form.
    • To create a customised form, select Quote Detail Form.
  4. Confirm the recipient's email address under To.
  5. Enter your email Subject and Message.
    • Alternatively, leave these blank to use the default subject and message as defined in System > Setup > Forms Setup > Quote > Email.
  6. Click View to preview the quote.
  7. Click Send > OK.

Go to System > Setup > Forms Setup > Quote > Display Options > Form Title to enter a new form title to display on the printable form document. The form will still retain its simPRO default title when you select it from the Form drop-down list.

You can also enter a new Estimate Form Title to display when you select Show as Estimate when emailing a quote form.

 Enable retention / retainage

You can enable retention / retainage in a project quote and set the amount.

To add retention to a project quote:

  1. Access the project quote as normal, then go to Details > Settings.
  2. Under Retention, click Enable Retention / Retainage.
  3. Enter the retention Period in months.
  4. Enter the Retention / Retainage Amount as a percentage.
  5. If desired, enter the Amount per Claim as a percentage.
  6. Enter the Final Claim Rebate as a percentage.
  7. Click Save.

When the project job has been invoiced, the retention will appear separately under Invoices > Retentions / Retainages.

 Advanced

Forecast

Use the Forecast tab to estimate the future of the quote.

To use the forecast tab:

  1. Access the project quote as normal, then go to Details > Forecast.
  2. Enter the Probability of Sale as a percentage.
  3. Select the Expected Due Date by selecting a month and year from the drop-down lists.
    • Alternatively, click This Month or Next Month.
  4. Click Save.
