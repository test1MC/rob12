Invoicing

Overview

In simPRO, an invoice is a list of goods sent or services provided and the sum due for these from your customer. After you have approved, emailed, and received payment for an invoice, you can apply the payment to the invoice in simPRO.

Use the Invoice Types diagram below to learn which invoice is applicable for your different projects.

Please ensure your job values are accurate and up to date prior to invoicing. Once you have created an invoice, you can only adjust values by beginning the audit process and voiding the invoice.

Create an invoice

There are several ways you can create an invoice for a job:

Update and invoice the job

Once you have finished updating a job that is ready to invoice, click Finish > Invoice Job.

Invoice an individual job

Go to Jobs > Pending / Progress / Complete, then click Options > Create Invoice on the job you wish to invoice. If you have already raised a progress claim against the job, click Options > Create Progress Claim.

Invoice multiple jobs at once

Go to Jobs > Complete to create individual invoices for multiple jobs with different customers at once.

Alternatively, go to Jobs > Pending / Progress / Complete to create one consolidated invoice for jobs that share the same customer and exchange rate.

Alternatively, go to Jobs > Pending / Progress / Complete to create one consolidated invoice for jobs that share the same customer and exchange rate.
Invoice multiple jobs at once

You can invoice several jobs at the same time to create multiple invoices for different customers.

To create an invoice for multiple jobs with different customers:

  1. Go to Jobs > Complete Jobs.
  2. Select the jobs you wish to invoice.
  3. Click Invoice / Invoice Separately > OK.
  4. Click Invoice Separately > OK.
  5. Click Invoice > OK.

Each invoice will individually appear in Invoices > Unpaid where you can continue creating the invoice.

You can only invoice multiple jobs with different customers when they are in the Complete stage.

 Create a consolidated invoice

You can create one consolidated invoice for multiple jobs that share the same customer and exchange rate.

To create a consolidated invoice:

  1. Go to Jobs > Pending / Progress / Complete Jobs.
  2. Select the check boxes for each job you would like to invoice.
  3. Click Invoice.
  4. Continue creating the invoice.

The selected jobs will be invoiced under the same invoice in Invoices > Unpaid. The Job No. column will display all jobs on the invoice.

When you email a consolidated invoice to the customer, select the Consolidated Invoice form in the Forms tab. This form is designed to show multiple jobs on one invoice and can be customised in System > Setup > Forms Setup > Invoice > Consolidated Invoice.

Invoice per item

Invoice a job per item to keep the cost centre/s unlocked and add additional billable items to the job. This is useful for 'do and charge' jobs that you need to continuously bill to your customers.

For example, you may wish to collect payments up front for your call out fees and other expenses, and then add billable items to the job once your technician is on site, or alternatively, you may be completing a 'do and charge' project job and you wish to bill for the items you have completed in the project.

If you choose to invoice per item for the first job invoice, all subsequent invoices will be invoiced per item.

When the job is complete and ready to be fully invoiced, you can invoice all items and lock the job.

To invoice a job per item:

  1. Ensure all job details are updated, then follow the invoice creation process as above.
  2. Ensure the Invoice Type is a Tax Invoice.
  3. Select the Date Issued.
  4. Under Payment Due, select the number of Days from invoice date or Days after EOM (end of month) that the payment is due.
    • This will automatically populate with the default set in System > Setup > Defaults > Financial, or in the customer card file.
  5. Select if the Invoice Stage is Pending or Approved.
    • This indicates your business's internal approval, not the customer's approval, and will depend on your security group permissions.
  6. Under Invoice Per Item, select Yes.
  7. Enter the Quantity Invoiced for each item as applicable.
  8. Enter an Invoice Comment and / or Footnote as desired.
    • Alternatively, select a script, or use the default Footnote defined in System > Setup > Forms Setup > Invoice > Footnote.
  9. Click Finish.

When you next access the job and go to Parts & Labour > Billable > Parts in the cost centres that have been invoiced, times, prices, and markup will be locked for items that have been invoiced to prevent price adjustments. You can still adjust item quantities, however they cannot be less than the quantity that has already been invoiced.

If you add items that have already been added and invoiced on the job, the additional items will appear in a new line in the Parts table.

Invoicing per item is not available for jobs with an adjusted price or with retention enabled. If you are completing a job in Connect that has previously been invoiced per item, you can invoice and apply payment to the remaining amount with the pricing and billable items restricted.

Invoice all items and lock the job

You need to ensure jobs are placed in the appropriate Jobs stage when invoicing per item:

Pending / Progress

The job is unlocked and you can continue adding items and invoicing per item.

Complete

The job is unlocked and you can continue adding items and invoicing per item. This stage is applicable for jobs that have been manually marked as Complete in Enterprise or Connect, but have not had all items invoiced.

If you invoice all items in Enterprise and / or Connect at this stage, the job will be locked and will move to Invoiced Jobs.

Invoiced

The job is locked after being marked as Complete and having all items invoiced in Enterprise and / or Connect.

If you unlock the job and add billable items, the job will move back to Complete Jobs where you can invoice the remaining items. You can also go to Jobs > Invoiced Jobs, then click Options > Job Stage > Pending / Progress on the desired job to move it to a different stage, unlock the cost centre, and continue adding and invoicing items.

Archived

The job is locked with all items invoiced and all payments applied in Enterprise and / or Connect.

For best practice, please ensure you move a job back to an active stage such as Pending / Progress / Complete before adding more items to an invoice.

To invoice all items and lock the job:

  1. Access the desired job, then go to Details > Settings.
  2. Under Stage, select Complete.
  3. Click Finish > Invoice Job.
  4. Click Invoice All for each cost centre.
  5. Click Finish.

The job will then be locked and you will not be able to create further invoices. This will also occur if you invoice all items on the job, then set the job stage to Complete at a different time, for example, when you click Options > Job Stage > Complete on a job in the Jobs table in Enterprise, or when you submit the job in Connect.

 Create a request for claim

If you want to send a customer a progress claim for a job, but first want to obtain their approval before posting invoice details to your accounting package, you can create a request for claim.

A request for claim is almost identical to a progress claim, but unlike a progress claim it does not post any information to your accounting package. This means you will not have to void a progress claim if your customer rejects your claim (providing only the percentage or value of the claim needs to change, and not the value of the project).

If a customer approves your request for claim, you can then easily convert it to a deposit or progress claim for payment.

To create a request for claim:

  1. Ensure all job details are updated, then follow the invoice creation process as above.
  2. Select Request for Claim as the Invoice Type.
  3. Select if the Invoice Stage is Pending or Approved.
    • This indicates your business's internal approval, not the customer's approval, and will depend on your security group permissions.
  4. Next to Claim Amount, enter the percentage or amount you wish to request to claim.
  5. Select and enter additional options and details as required.
  6. Click Finish.

If the request for claim has been marked as internally Approved, click Forms to email the request for claim to the customer.

When emailing a request for claim to a customer, you can use a unique form known as a Payment Schedule form. It displays project information such as total contract value, claims to date, and the claim value remaining. It also shows a payment schedule table including project progress, and the claim amount for customer approval.

Convert a request for claim to progress claim

After a customer approves your request for claim, you can convert it to a deposit or progress claim to send to the customer.

To convert a request for claim approved by a customer:

  1. Locate the relevant job.
  2. Click next to Options on the job you wish to approve a claim for.
  3. Under Unpaid Invoices, click the desired request for claim.
    • Alternatively, on the request for claim, click Options > View Invoice.
  4. Click Unlock in the top right corner.
  5. Select Progress Claim as the Invoice Type.
  6. Make additional changes to the progress claim as desired.
  7. Click Finish.

If the Invoice Stage of the new progress claim is marked as Approved, click the Forms tab to email the new invoice as normal.

 Create a progress claim

Raise progress claims to invoice for part of a job, such as when one stage of a project has been completed. When creating a claim, you can enter a percentage of the total value to claim, or manually enter a claim amount. Progress claims are numbered in order of issue, so the first claim you raise will be named Progress Claim #1 and subsequent claims will be labelled as #2, #3 etc, while the final progress claim will be labelled as Final Claim. Progress claims may be associated with retentions.

You can create a progress claim for a pending, progress or completed job and claim per item or enter the decided Claim Amount value as a percentage or an absolute figure. This amount can be claimed at section level or cost centre level. If you choose to claim by value, the Claim Per Item option will not be available for any subsequent progress claims for that job.

There are several tools you can use in simPRO to assist in calculating your claim amounts so that you maintain positive cash flow:

Jobs Work In Progress (WIP) Report

Generate the report to view a total summary of costs and current billing for a job.

Financial View

When creating a progress claim for a project job, access the desired job, then go to Project Overview > Projected and select Financial View to view the current budget and actual margin in a breakdown of costs incurred and claims to date.

Claim Report

When creating a progress claim, generate the Claim Report to view the recommended minimum claim based on the costs incurred up to a specified date.

To generate the report, create a progress claim as normal, then select when you wish to Include Costs To and click View Report above the claimable sections of the job. If you select to Include Costs To a future date, any costs not currently incurred but are due for receipt prior to that date will be included in the report.

To create a progress claim:​

  1. Ensure all job details are updated, then follow the invoice creation process as above.
  2. Select Progress Claim as the Invoice Type.
  3. Select the Date Issued.
  4. Under Payment Due, select the number of Days from invoice date or Days after EOM (end of month) that the payment is due.
    • This will automatically populate with the default set in System > Setup > Defaults > Financial, or in the customer card file.
  5. Select if the Invoice Stage is Pending or Approved.
    • This indicates your business's internal approval, not the customer's approval, and will depend on your security group permissions.
  6. Enter the desired Claim Amount for each cost centre as a percentage or absolute figure, or alternatively, click Claim All.
    • To claim a percentage of an entire section, enter the Claim percentage in the grey bar below the section name.
  7. Click Finish.

You can then email the progress claim to the customer from the Forms tab. You can adjust how the progress claim appears in System > Setup > Forms Setup > Invoice > Display Options > Claims.

Select Yes from the Claim Per Item drop-down list.

  1. Ensure all job details are updated, then follow the invoice creation process as above.
  2. Select Progress Claim as the Invoice Type.
  3. Select the Date Issued.
  4. Under Payment Due, select the number of Days from invoice date or Days after EOM (end of month) that the payment is due.

Raise the final progress claim

On the final claim of the project, click Finalise Claim to ensure all remaining cost centres are claimed, then click Finish. The retention amount will be added to the final invoice.

 View the claim summary

The progress claim summary provides an overview of the cost centres and sections claimed to date, the progress claims created and paid off, and the remaining claim balance.

To view the claim summary, you need to enable it in your invoice form display options. You can then generate the desired progress claim, go to Details > Forms, select the desired Form, then click View.

You can then view the following below the Balance Due:

Original Contract

The Description and Total Value for each project section, as well as the percentage or value claimed for each section in the Current claim and To Date.

Claim Breakdown

The Date, Invoice No., Claimed and Paid amounts, and the Amount Outstanding for every progress claim raised against the job.

If a credit note has been raised against a previous progress claim, it will apply in the Paid column.

Contract Summary

The Gross Claim to Date and the Remaining Claim Balance for the job.

Go to System > Setup > Forms Setup > Invoice > Display Options > Claims to adjust how the claim summary displays, including whether to include cost centres with a zero value sell price.

 Create a retention / retainage claim

Retention, also known as retainage in the United States, is the percentage of payment held from you by the customer to safeguard against defects in workmanship and ensure the job is completed to specific standards. Retention / retainage settings are usually defined at the beginning of a project to ensure amounts are calculated correctly for each progress claim.

After the project has been completed, all cost centres have been claimed, and the defined retention / retainage period, also known as a defects liability period, has passed, you will receive an alert in simPRO to claim the outstanding retention / retainage. You can then can create a retention / retainage claim to receive your defined final claim rebate.

To create a retention / retainage claim:

  1. Go to Invoices > Retentions / Retainages.
  2. Click Options > Create Retention / Retainage Claim on the desired outstanding retention / retainage.
  3. Adjust the Date Issued, Payment Due terms and Invoice Comment as necessary.
  4. View all retentions / retainages held or rebated in the Retentions / Retainages Held table, then adjust the Retention / Retainage Claimed amount as necessary.
  5. Click Finish.

You can then email the retention / retainage claim to the customer from the Forms tab.

After the project has been completed, all cost centres have been claimed, and the defined retention / retainage period, also known as a defects liability period, has passed, you will receive an alert in simPRO to claim the outstanding retention / retainage. You can then can create a retention / retainage claim to receive your defined final claim rebate.

To create a retention / retainage claim:

  1. Go to Invoices > Retentions / Retainages.
  2. Click Options > Create Retention / Retainage Claim on the desired outstanding retention / retainage.
  3. Adjust the Date Issued, Payment Due terms and Invoice Comment as necessary.
  4. View all retentions / retainages held or rebated in the Retentions / Retainages Held table, then adjust the Retention / Retainage Claimed amount as necessary.
  5. Click Finish.

You can then email the retention / retainage claim to the customer from the Forms tab.

 Email an invoice

To email an invoice:

  1. Access the desired invoice, then click Forms.
  2. Select the invoice Form you wish to send.
    • Only the forms you have assigned in System > Setup > Forms Setup > Invoice > Forms will be available to select.
  3. Under To, enter the recipient's email address, or alternatively, select a different Primary Contact, or click to add additional Contacts.
  4. The primary contact for invoicing as defined in the customer card file will automatically populate this field.
  5. Adjust the default Subject and Body if desired. The default is set up in System > Setup > Forms Setup > Invoice / Request for Claim.
    • Alternatively, click Clear Email to enter a new Subject and Body.
  6. Click View to preview your invoice before you send it.
  7. Click Send > OK > OK.

If you need to send multiple invoices, use the Aged Receivables report. Select the customers you want to email a statement to and click Email in the footer options.

It recommended that you do not generate .docx when you click View as it allows you to alter the invoice outside of simPRO. This can cause errors within your accounting system and impact reporting.

To assign forms:

  1. Go to System > Setup > Forms Setup > Invoice / Request For Claim > Forms.
  2. Select the form you wish to assign from the Forms Assigned drop-down list, then click Add Form.

Adjust progress claim display options

Go to System > Setup > Forms Setup > Invoice > Display Options > Claim to specify how claimed values are displayed on progress claim forms.

You may wish to generate a preview of a progress claim in the Forms tab to view how your settings apply. Each time you make a change, click Save, then refresh the browser tab containing the progress claim form.

You can then adjust the following display options:

Progress Claim Shows

Select to show All Cost Centres, All Cost Centres Excluding $0 / £0 Values, Only Cost Centres Claimed on Current Invoice, or Only Cost Centres Claimed To Date.

You may wish to select to display All Cost Centres Excluding $0 / £0 Values if you are using a cost centre or section with a zero value sell price to manage labour and perform other internal tasks for the job that are not relevant to the customer.

Show Contract Table Summary for Claims

Select Off to display only the invoice body. Select On to include the claim summary below the invoice body, or select Always Show On New Page to include the claim summary below the invoice body on a new page.

Claim Summary Lists By

Select to display claim summary values by Section or Cost Centre.

Variation Summary Lists By

Select to display variation summary values by Section or Cost Centre.

Claim / Variation Summary Shows

Select All Cost Centres to display a breakdown of all cost centres on the invoiced job.

Select All Cost Centres Excluding $0 / £0 Values to display a breakdown of all cost centres on the invoiced job with a value of more than zero. This is useful if you are using a cost centre or section with a zero value sell price to manage labour and perform other internal tasks for the job that are not relevant to the customer.

Select Only Cost Centres Claimed On Current Invoice to display a breakdown of only the cost centres currently being claimed.

Claim Summary Shows

Claim Breakdwon Table

 Adjust the How to Pay section

You can customise how the How To Pay section appears on a customer invoice in your invoice Forms Setup, including which payment options are available and how they are formatted.

To view how your changes apply, access any invoice, then go to the Forms tab and click View to open a preview of the invoice in a new tab. As you make adjustments in the How To Pay settings, click Save, then refresh the tab with your invoice preview to view your changes.

To adjust the How To Pay section:

  1. Go to System > Setup > Forms Setup > Invoices > How To Pay.
  2. Under How To Pay:
    • Adjust the How To Pay Section Height, and select whether to include a How To Pay Separator, Title, and Invoice No.
    • Enter a Payment Claim Message if desired.
    • AU and NZ only: Enter your BPay Biller Code and select whether your BPay Accepts Credit Card.
  3. Select the desired payment methods and details you wish to show from the Options Assigned drop-down list, then click Add Fields.
    • Click and drag the grey drag handle to reposition the payment methods and details.
  4. If applicable, adjust options as desired to customise how the Credit Card, Mail, and Direct Deposit payment methods appear.
  5. Click Save.

 Create and set up a claim statement for requests for claim

To create and set up a claim statement for requests for claim:

  2. Enter the desired Statement Section Height.
  3. If desired, select whether the Statement Separator is On or Off. This adds a dotted line to separate the statement from the form content.
  4. Enter your statement in the Payment Claim Statement field.
  5. Click Save.
