Receipt a Purchase Order

Overview

While a purchase order gives a committed cost to stock, quotes and jobs, receipting a purchase order will finalise the actual cost of those items. You can create receipts for purchase orders to suit your workflow, depending on whether you want to create a receipt after receiving goods, create a receipt after receiving goods and a site invoice, or create a receipt before receiving goods.

Once you enter site invoice details into a receipt, simPRO will flag the invoice to be posted the next time you export to your accounting package. Ensure all item costs, freight, tax and totals match the site's invoice exactly.

Receipt a purchase order Receipt a purchase order after receiving goods

To receipt a purchase order after receiving goods:

  1. Go to Materials > Purchase Orders.
  2. On the purchase order you wish to receipt, click Options > Create a Receipt.
    • Alternatively, open the purchase order and click the Receipts sub-tab.
  3. Enter a Packing Slip No., ensure Items Received ? is selected, and adjust other details as required.
  4. Click Finish.

When you receive a site invoice for the goods, open this receipt under Materials > Receipts, click Unlock, enter an Invoice No., and edit the Cost Centres and Unit Prices as required.

 Receipt a purchase order after receiving goods and a site invoice

To receipt a purchase order after receiving a site invoice:

  1. Go to Materials > Purchase Orders.
  2. On the purchase order you wish to receipt, click Options > Create a Receipt.
    • Alternatively, open the purchase order and click the Receipts sub-tab.
  3. Enter the site's Invoice No., enter a Packing Slip No., ensure Items Received ? is selected, adjust items' Cost Centres and Unit Prices, and adjust other details as required.
  4. Click Finish.
 Receipt a purchase order before receiving goods

If you need to pay for goods in advance (such as for a prepaid purchase or a pro forma), you can receipt a purchase order before receiving the goods.

To receipt a purchase order in advance:

  1. Go to Materials > Purchase Orders.
  2. On the purchase order you wish to receipt, click Options > Create a Receipt.
  3. Alternatively, open the purchase order and click the Receipts sub-tab.
  4. Enter the site's Invoice No., adjust items' Cost Centres and Unit Prices, clear the Items Received ? check box, and adjust other details as required.
  5. Click Finish.

When you receive the goods, open this receipt under Materials > Receipts, click Unlock, select Items Received ?, and enter a Packing Slip No. as applicable.

 Advanced Select an item's cost centre

When receipting a purchase order, you can select a Cost Centre for each item to ensure their costs are posted to the correct account in your accounting package. For example, if you purchase a selection of wires to install on a job and order a tool at the same time, you may want to select a separate cost centre for the tool.

When Inventory Tracking is enabled, each inventory item will have Inventory as the selected cost centre.

 Bulk set invoice quantities to zero

If you wish to receipt a purchase order, but you have only received quantities for one of the line items, you can set all other line item quantities to zero in bulk.

To bulk set invoice quantities to zero:

  1. Go to Materials > Purchase Orders.
  2. On the purchase order you wish to receipt, click Options > Create a Receipt.
    • Alternatively, open the purchase order and click the Receipts sub-tab.
  3. Under Stock Ordered, select the line items you do not wish to receipt, then click Set Qty to Zero.
    • Alternatively, select Select All, then click Set Qty to Zero to set all invoice quantities to zero.
  4. Continue receipting the purchase order as normal.
 Adjust tax

In some cases your site may calculate rounding on tax differently to simPRO.

Use the arrows in the Tax Adjustment field to make slight adjustments to the tax, such as rounding cents up or down.

 Enter a tax amount (US only)

In US versions of simPRO, you can enter a tax amount when receipting a purchase order.

To use this feature you must first enter a Tax Account under System > Setup > Defaults > Financial.

When creating or editing a receipt, enter an amount in the Tax field. The Tax: total at the bottom will update accordingly.

If the items already have taxes assigned to them, entering an amount in the Tax field will add to this value.

If you enter a Freight cost, the tax value will need to include tax on freight too.
