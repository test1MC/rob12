Release Notes

What's new in simPRO Enterprise and Service: from 30 October 2016 AEST​

V16.4.4

New features in Enterprise and Service Sneak peek (click to zoom) Learn how it works (click Full Screen)

Progress claims now contain a comprehensive claim breakdown showing details for all progress claims already raised against the job. Your customers will be able to see dates, amounts, any payments applied, and the amounts still outstanding for each claim.

In another improvement to progress claims, if you’re using any cost centres or sections with a zero value sell price to track costs, manage labour or assist with any other internal tasks, you can now exclude those values from the progress claim you send your customer.

If you use the UPC (Universal Product Code) and Country of Origin fields for your catalogue items, good news! Those fields can now be captured and included when importing (or exporting) generic catalogues.

Fixes and changes

  • Resolved issues with job scripts and descriptions.
  • Sometimes multiple alerts were displaying for a single stock item – now you’ll only be warned once. Did you get that? Now you’ll only be warned once!
  • Documents attached in Forms Setup were mysteriously absent from the Main Menu > Documents menu. They’ll now appear, ready for use!
  • Seeing double? It’s not your eyesight – contracts, customers and site information were sometimes displaying twice. They’ll stop repeating themselves now.

What's new in simPRO Enterprise and Service: from 30 October 2016 AEST​

V16.4.3

New features in Enterprise and Service Sneak peek (click to zoom) Learn how it works (click Full Screen)

Do your customers say ‘Invoice’ or ‘Tax Invoice’? You can now use the terminology that suits your region or business by adjusting the title a customer sees on your form PDFs, including invoices, quotes, purchase orders, statements, and more.

Want to add extra details to your catalogue items? Now you have the option to add a Universal Product Code and a Country of Origin.

 The lead summary form is now even more useful, displaying the date and time of anyone who’s been scheduled to that lead.

Fixes and changes

  • If you remove an invoice from a job, that job’s stage will now revert to ‘Complete’ rather than inaccurately staying as ‘Invoiced’.
  • In the simPOS add-on, the stage of jobs that were invoiced and paid would not update from ‘Complete’ to ‘Invoiced’ – they’ll now show the correct stage.
  • The service level field went temporarily AWOL on the asset register table in forms – it’s back again now.
  • If you use a trade price pricing tier on a project job, the Service Fee and Callout Fee options will once again be available to add to the job.

What's new in simPRO Enterprise and Service: from 16 October 2016 AEST​

V16.4.2

New features in Enterprise and Service Sneak peek (click to zoom) Learn how it works (click Full Screen)

 Invoicing’s now flexible to suit your workflow! When you create a tax invoice, you can invoice per item and keep the job unlocked. This means you’re free to add additional items to the job as work progresses.

When you schedule using Project View, you can remove a whole week’s worth of schedules with one click. Gone is the need to remove each schedule individually, saving you loads of time!  

 If you do business in Costa Rica, you can now select the appropriate currency with the addition of the Costa Rican colón. Bravo!

 

Fixes and changes

  • Want to know exactly who’s logged in to Enterprise or Service at any one time? The User Log on your dashboard will now show you a list of employees currently logged in to simPRO, as well as the times when they logged in and were last active.
  • To open a simPRO link in a new tab in your browser, simply press the CTRL button while clicking the link. CTRL+C this to your memory!
  • Reports: Recurring invoices were not appearing in the Contract Profitability report. We’re happy to report they’ll be back again. (and again…)
  • Xero: Credits created via a negative dollar job were failing to transfer to Xero. Our apologies, they’ll now transfer as they should.
  • With the improved Sales Invoices report, you can see invoices for both active and archived customers in a specified date range. Too easy!

What's new in simPRO Enterprise and Service: from 3 October 2016 AEST​

V16.4.1

New features in Enterprise and Service Sneak peek (click to zoom) Learn how it works (click Full Screen)

When you’re emailing a form – like a quote to a customer, or a purchase order to a supplier – you can now have more choice over what message is used, with the ability to preview the email and even edit it before sending.

Use a lot of plant items or equipment in your business? No more manually creating entries for each item – save time by importing a list (you can now export a list, too!). Make sure you update your security group permissions so you can import and export away!

Fixes and changes

  • A bug was including job variation sections in tax calculations even when the 'Include in retention calculations' check box was not selected. This meant you may have claimed tax on sections you didn't intend to. We have fixed this error and apologise for any inconvenience caused.
  • After successfully posting transactions across to an integrated Xero accounting package, the transactions will now be correctly marked as flagged.
  • As you can only create a system snapshot every four hours, we’ve fixed a bug that was counting deleting a snapshot as the creation of a new one.
  • When posting invoices to an integrated QuickBooks Online accounting package, having an apostrophe in a customer’s name will no longer give you the “Customer cannot be found” error message. Phe’w!
  • The Lead Status and Lead List reports won’t be returning simPRO errors any more.
  • We’ve had a word with our Quote Detail Form Summary tables, so they’ll now show the right figures when using Primary and Options sections.
