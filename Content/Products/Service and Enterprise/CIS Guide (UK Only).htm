CIS Guide (UK Only)

Overview

If you do business in the UK, you can use simPRO to apply CIS deduction rates to contractor and customer invoices.

This article only provides instructions for using the CIS features in simPRO, and should not be interpreted as legal or financial advice. Consult the UK Government website for information on your CIS obligations.

Set up CIS in simPRO

Before you can use simPRO's CIS-related features, you must first enable them, then enter some important details.

Enable CIS features

To enable CIS in simPRO:

  1. Go to System > Setup > Defaults > Extensions.
  2. Under System Extensions > CIS, select On.
  3. Click Save.
 Enter CIS account numbers and deduction rate

The CIS Tax Suffered Account relates to sales invoices, and the CIS Liability Account relates to contractors.

To enter your CIS account numbers and deduction rate:

  1. Go to System > Setup > Defaults > Financial.
  2. Under Accounts, enter the CIS Tax Suffered Account and CIS Liability Account numbers from your accounting package.
  3. Under Invoicing, select the applicable CIS Deduction Rate from the drop-down list. This selection will become the system default, but you can manually change it if necessary.
  4. Click Save.
 Ensure your company details are accurate

Before using the CIS features in simPRO, ensure your company details are accurate.

To check your company details:

  1. Go to System > Setup > Company.
  2. Update any necessary fields, particularly your UTR #.
  3. Click Save.
 Enter a contractor's UTR number

You may have entered the UTR number when creating the contractor's card file. Each contractor needs to have this number recorded in their card files.

To enter a contractor's UTR number:

  1. Open the contractor's card file, or create a new one.
  2. Go to Profile > Details.
  3. Enter the contractor's UTR Number.
  4. Click Save or Finish.
 Assign a CIS deduction rate to a contractor

You can assign a CIS deduction rate to an individual contractor, and that rate will automatically apply. You will still be able to manually adjust this rate if required.

To assign a CIS deduction rate to a specific contractor:

  1. Open the contractor's card file, or create a new one.
  2. Go to the Rates tab.
  3. Select the applicable CIS Deduction Rate from the drop-down list.
  4. Click Save or Finish.
 Apply a CIS deduction rate to a contractor invoice

To apply a CIS deduction rate to a contractor invoice:

  1. Invoice a contractor work order as usual.
  2. CIS Deduction will appear in the pricing breakdown below. If the contractor has a CIS deduction rate assigned to their card file, it will apply here. You can manually edit this value if required.
  3. Complete the invoice as usual.
Contractor invoices and your accounting package

When you create a contractor invoice, the invoice will not appear in your accounting link to send to your accounting package unless you enter a Contractor Invoice No. Once you have entered this number and clicked Save or Finish, the invoice will appear in your accounting link, regardless of whether it has been paid or not.

 Apply a CIS deduction rate to a customer invoice

To apply a CIS deduction rate to a customer invoice:

  1. Create an invoice as usual.
  2. Select the applicable rate from the CIS Deduction Rate drop-down list.
  3. Less CIS Deduction will appear in the pricing breakdown below. You can manually edit the value if required.
  4. Complete the invoice as usual.

CIS deductions against customer invoices go through to your accounting package as credit notes. This reduces the liability of the customer and shows what money has been withheld.

Always deduct CIS for a specific customer

If you need to always deduct CIS on all quotes and jobs for a specific customer, you can select this option on the customer's card file. If this is selected, you will not need to select a CIS Deduction Rate when creating an invoice for this customer, and the standard CIS deduction rate will automatically be applied. You will still be able to manually adjust this rate if required.

To assign a CIS deduction rate to a specific customer:

  1. Open the customer's card file, or create a new one.
  2. Go to the Rates tab.
  3. Under Always Deduct CIS?, select Yes. The default CIS deduction rate will be used for this customer's invoices.
  4. Click Save or Finish.
 View reports

View CIS Deductions report

You can use the CIS Deductions report to view CIS deducted by each customer in a specified date range.

View CIS Monthly Returns report

The CIS Monthly Returns report can help provide information you need when filing your monthly report with HMRC, and you can also use it to generate a CIS Payment and Deduction Statement to send to sub-contractors.

 Tips

Modify table view for contractor invoices

You can modify your table view in Contractor Unpaid Invoices and Contractor Paid Invoices to include fields for VAT Amount, CIS Deduction, Payment Amount etc.

Simply go to Invoices > Contractor Unpaid / Paid Invoices, then click Modify Table View. Select Show for the fields you wish to view, then click Finish.

To download paid and unpaid invoice information, click the download icon in the top right, then click CSV.
