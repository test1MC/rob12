Managing Optional Quotes

Overview

Use optional cost centres in a quote to provide your customer with options. You can add optional cost centres as an alternative or in addition to your primary, billable cost centres. Only primary cost centres will be included on the job once the quote is converted.

For example, you may wish to offer a customer a choice between a cheaper option and more expensive option for the same requested works. In this case, you may wish to add only optional cost centres to the quote. You can then email the quote form to the customer and they can select their desired quote, which you can then make billable in simPRO and convert to the job.

You can also use optional cost centres as an upsell opportunity to offer customers an extra product or service in addition to the requested works. For example, a customer may request a quote for an installation, which you would add as a primary cost centre. However, you may also wish to offer them a quote for an additional installation, which you could then add as an optional cost centre. Then, if the upsell is successful, you can make the optional cost centre billable and convert the entire quote to a job.

Add optional cost centres to a quote

You can add optional cost centres to both project quotes and service quotes with multiple cost centres in simPRO Enterprise.

To add optional cost centres to a quote:

  1. Access or create the quote as normal.
  2. Go to Cost Centre List > Options.
  3. For project quotes, enter the section Name and Description as desired.
  4. Select the desired Cost Centres, then click Add.
  5. Repeat as necessary for each cost centre.
  6. Click each cost centre to Manage parts and labour in a service quote / Add parts and labour to a project quote as normal.

After you have finalised the applicable cost centres for the quote, you can then email the quote form to the customer for them to choose the quoted cost centres they wish to proceed with.

Set up quote forms with optional cost centres

You can adjust how your optional cost centres appear on the quote forms you send to customers.

You may wish to test how different settings appear by accessing a quote with optional cost centres, then going to Details > Forms, selecting Quote - Itemised / Quote - Itemised w/Prices, then clicking View. When you make a change to the form setup, click Save, then refresh the page in which the form is open.

To set up display options for optional cost centres on standard quote forms:

  1. Go to System > Setup > Forms Setup > Quote > Display Options > General.
  2. Select On under Show Optional Summary if desired.
    • You also need to select On under Show Summary for the optional summary to appear on the form.
  3. Under Sections / Cost Centres / Materials/Labour, select Same For Options to use the same settings for both primary and optional cost centres on the form.
    • Alternatively, clear the check box to select different settings as desired.
  4. Adjust other display options as desired.
  5. Click Save.

To display optional cost centres on the Quote Detail Form, create or access a detail form template as desired, then add the Optional Cost Centre Item Lists block.

You can then email the customers the desired quote form from a service quote or project quote as normal. You can then Mark optional cost centres as billable if the customer has chosen to proceed with them before converting the quote to a job.

Mark optional cost centres as billable

To make optional cost centres billable on a quote to include them in the converted job, go to Cost Centre List > Options, then click Options > Mark as Billable on the desired cost centre. Alternatively, you can access the desired optional cost centre, then go to Details > Settings > Cost Centre Type and select Chargeable.

The cost centre will then appear in the Primary sub-tab, and will be included when you convert the quote to a job.

To mark a billable cost centre as optional, go to Cost Centre List > Primary, then click Options > Mark as Optional on the desired cost centre, or access the desired primary cost centre, then go to Details > Settings > Cost Centre Type and select Optional.

You need to mark optional cost centres as billable before converting the applicable quote to a job. Optional cost centres are not accessible at the job level.
