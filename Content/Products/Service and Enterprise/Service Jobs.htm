Service Jobs

Overview

Create 'do and charge' or service jobs in simPRO. When you create a service job, you can also schedule technicians and, in simPRO Enterprise, contractors, send job forms, add labour and materials, and raise purchase orders.

Simplified service job workflow

Create a service job

You can create a service job from the Jobs menu, from the schedule Day View / Week View, or from within the customer / site card file. You can also convert an quote into a job, so that all of the information already entered will carry over.

To create a service job from the Jobs menu:

  1. Go to Jobs > Create New Job / Create New Service Job.
  2. Search and select a Customer and Site from the drop-down lists.
    • Alternatively, click Create New to create a new customer and / or site.
  3. Select a Customer Contact and Site Contact, or click Create New to create new contacts.
    • The contact phone numbers will then be available in Connect. The Site Contact may be a store manager, building manager etc.
  4. Select the desired Cost Centre.
    • Cost centres separate operational costs and services within your business to assist in reporting.
  5. Enter an Order Number and Request Number if required.
  6. Enter the job Description to appear on the job card, in Connect, and on the customer invoice.​​
    • Alternatively, select a script from the Insert Script drop-down list. You can then adjust the description as desired using the text formatting options.
  7. Assign a Salesperson and / or Project Manager and enter a Due Date if desired.
    • You need to mark employees as Salesperson / Project Manager in their card files to select them.
  8. Assign a Salesperson and enter a Due Date if desired.
    • You need to mark employees as Salesperson in their card files to select them.
  9. Assign a Salesperson and / or Project Manager and enter a Due Date if desired.
    • You need to mark employees as Salesperson / Project Manager in their card files to select them.
  10. Select Tags to categorise the job.
  11. Schedule a technician to the job using the options in the grey area to the right.
    • Alternatively, you can schedule the job in the Schedule View or in the Schedule tab of the job after creating it.
    • You may wish to assign a Technician and a Due Date under the job Description instead of scheduling to allow the technician to respond to and complete a job at their earliest convenience. If you both assign and schedule a technician, the job will appear twice in Connect.
  12. Click Finish to create and exit the job.
    • Alternatively, click Next to continue building the job.

When you have clicked Finish on a service job, you can choose to:

View Jobs View all jobs in the Pending / Progress Jobs table, depending on how you have set up your automatic triggers in System > Setup > System Setup > Status Codes > Automatic Triggers.
Edit Job Go to the Job Summary to continue building the job.
Schedule Job Schedule the job to a technician in the Day View / Week View, depending on your settings in System > Setup > Defaults > Schedule > Default View.
Invoice Job Invoice the job.

You can return to the Job Setup page to make changes by accessing the desired job, then going to Details > Settings > Job Setup, or by clicking the beginning of the breadcrumb at the top of the job.

 Schedule a service job

There are several ways to schedule service jobs to specific technicians in simPRO:

  • In Job Setup when you create a service job.
  • In the Schedule tab of a service job.
  • In the schedule Day View / Week View / Manual View.

Before you begin scheduling service jobs, you may wish to use a number of simPRO scheduling tools to increase scheduling accuracy and efficiency.

Schedule a service job in Job Setup

To schedule a service job:

  1. Create a job as normal.
  2. In the grey area to the right, enter the Job Duration, then search and select the applicable Zone.
  3. Under Preferred Time, click a time allotment in the AM or PM of the desired day.
    • ​​Select Hide Weekends or click the blue arrows to alter the schedule view as desired.
  4. Click Calculate to filter Technician(s) to Schedule by Travel Distance to the job site.
  5. Enter a Start Time and / or Finish Time for the desired technician(s), then click Select.
  6. Click Finish to create and exit the job or click Next to continue building the job.
    • ​​If you have accessed Job Setup from Details > Settings within the job, click Update to save your changes.

 Schedule a service job from the Schedule tab

Go to Schedule in a created service job to schedule technicians and, in simPRO Enterprise, teams to the job.

To schedule a job in the Schedule tab:

  1. Access the desired job, then go to Schedule.
  2. Under Available Resources, click the calendar icon next to the employee, or in simPRO Enterprise, contractor / team you wish to schedule.
    • Click an employee / contractor / team to view their available hours over the next five days.
  3. In the top left of the schedule view, select the desired schedule rate.
  4. Click the desired time block to add a schedule block.
    • Click and drag the schedule block to move it to a different day or hour.
    • Click and drag the top and bottom edges to lengthen or shorten the schedule block.
  5. When you have finished scheduling, click the close icon in the top right.
  6. Click Save.

Alternatively, click Help me decide to schedule a job using the same Filter Options as the Job Setup page.

If the quantity of hours scheduled is different to the quantity of hours being billed in Parts & Labour, or the scheduled labour rate type is different to the labour rate being billed to the customer, a warning will appear. Click Update Time to match billed time to that of the scheduled hours and labour rate including time associated with service fees.

 View the job summary

Go to Details > Summary in a service job to adjust the job description, enter private notes visible in Connect but not visible to the customer, manage the total costs of associated parts and labour, and view a timeline of all job activity in simPRO.

In the remaining Details sub-tabs, you can adjust job settings, email a job form to the customer, upload job attachments, and view a log of the job history. In simPRO Enterprise, you can also create job tasks and assign contractors.

Within any sub-tab, click the black drop-down arrow in the top right to view customer and site details, the job total, the assigned technician if applicable and the cost centre description.

Adjust job settings

Go to Details > Settings in a service job to adjust the overall settings for the job and access the following options:

SMS Job If you have registered for and set up SMS in simPRO, you can send SMS to mobile devices directly from the job.
Copy Job Create an identical copy of the service job with a different job number and creation date.
Job Setup Return to the Job Setup page to change the customer and / or site for the job.

You can also enter a Request No. supplied by the customer to add additional reference information to the job, as well as an Order No. if required by the customer.

If desired, adjust the date when the job was Created, as well as the Due Date / Time to receive system alerts. You can also assign a Technician to the job, as well as a Salesperson and, in simPRO Enterprise, Manager if the relevant employees have been designated in their employee card files.

To manually move the job to a different Stage or change the Status, select as applicable from the drop-down list. Alternatively, select Auto Adjust to use automatic triggers.

Search and select to apply project Tags to the job, and select a different Cost Centre if desired. You can also enter information in Custom Fields as set up in System > Setup > System Setup > Custom Fields > Leads, Quotes, Jobs & Recurring.

Jobs will appear twice in Connect if you both assign and schedule the applicable technician.

 Create a contractor work order

Go to Details > Contractors in a service job to create a contractor work order. Once the contractor has completed and invoiced the job, you can record the invoice in simPRO.

Alternatively, you can schedule a contractor in the Job Setup page or Schedule tab to automatically create a work order with the scheduled hours automatically applied.

If the contractor works for an hourly rate, this should be defined in the Rates tab of the contractor card file.

 Manage parts and labour in a service job

Go to Parts & Labour > Billable to manage all billable and non-billable parts and labour in a service job. You can Add a service fee, Add catalogue items, pre-builds and take off templates (take off templates are only available in simPRO Enterprise), Create and add one off Items, and Manage labour in a service job. In simPRO Enterprise, you can also link a supplier quote to the job by selecting a Supplier and Supplier Quote, then clicking Apply.

In the Parts table, you can adjust the estimated Time for an item, and in simPRO Enterprise, you can also select a Fit Time multiplier. When adjusting Time for items, remember this will affect the Estimated Time and Time Billed in the Labour table.

You can adjust the Price, Markup, Sell Price and Qty of items. To reset the Price, select the check box or click Options > Reset Pricing.

Clicking Options will also allow you to open and edit an item, remove it, change the tax code or make it non-billable.

Add a service fee

In simPRO, a service fee is an additional fee you charge on a job, such as a call-out fee, with or without labour included. Service fees set as a default in System Setup > Labour > Service Fees or the customer card file will be automatically added to a service job in Details > Parts & Labour > Parts.

To add a service fee to a service job:

  1. Access the desired job.
  2. Go to Parts & Labour > Billable.
  3. Select a Call out / Service Fee, then click Add.
  4. In the Parts table, adjust the Time, Markup / Sell Price and Qty of the service fee.
  5. Click Options to remove the fee, reset pricing, change the tax code or make the fee non-billable.
  6. Click Save or Finish.

Click Options > Remove Item to remove a service fee from a service job. Alternatively, select the service fee check box, then click Remove under the table.

  4. Within each group / subgroup, enter the desired quantity per item, then click Add Items.
    • Alternatively, click Create Pre-Build / Create Item to create a new pre-build / catalogue item.
  5. Click Billable to
  6. Click Billable to Manage parts and labour in a service job as normal.
    • Click Options > Edit Pre-Build On Job to adjust the materials in the pre-build only in that job. The original pre-build quantity will not be affected.
  7. Click Save or Finish.
 Create and add one off Items

Create one off items to cost non-catalogue expenses such as parking, government charges, and custom-engineered tools to a service job.

To create a one off item in a service job:

  1. Access the desired job, then go to Parts & Labour > One Off Items.
  2. Click Add One Off Item.
  3. Select the item Type.
  4. Select Billable to add the item to the customer invoice.
  5. Select Inventory Item to be able to order and track the item as you Manage stock within a service job.
  6. Enter a detailed item Description.
  7. Enter the Estimated Cost and Markup % / Sell Price.
    • If the item has associated labour, enter an Estimated Time in minutes.
  8. Enter the required Quantity.
  9. Select a Sales Tax Code.
  10. Click Finish.
  11. Click Billable to Manage parts and labour in a service job as normal.
  12. Click Save or Finish.

If the actual cost of an item becomes known while a job is in progress, go to Parts & Labour > One Off Items in the desired job, then enter the Actual Cost and the Actual Cost Date upon which the cost was incurred.

 Manage labour in a service job

A labour rate that has been marked as default in the customer card file will be automatically selected in the Labour table when the job is created. To add a labour rate, select as desired from the Labour drop-down list, then click Add. You can then adjust the Markup, Sell Price and Time as desired.

You can also adjust labour automatically to be based on hours added when you Schedule a service job from the Schedule tab. If the quantity of hours scheduled is different to the quantity of hours being billed in Parts & Labour, or the scheduled labour rate type is different to the labour rate being billed to the customer, you will be prompted to update the billable labour from the Schedule tab.

Click Options on a labour rate to remove it or change the tax code if desired.

You need to assign labour rates in the customer card file to add them to a job for the customer.

 Manage stock within a service job

Go to Stock > All in a service job to manage all required or allocated stock items. You can filter stock items according to Storage Location (storage device), or alternatively, perform a local search by entering the part number, description or search terms, then clicking Search.

In Stock > Required, you can manage required items that have not yet been ordered or allocated from stock. These are items that have been added as you Manage parts and labour in a service job.

In Stock > Allocated, manage items that have been assigned to the job. This is particularly useful for adjusting overallocation of stock.

Click the black arrows or enter quantities in the Assigned column to adjust quantities assigned to the service job. You cannot assign more items than are Required. When you have finished making changes, click Update Stock > Save. Then, go to Billable > Parts to ensure your changes have been reflected.

To view, print, email or attach a list of all stock items assigned to a service job during a selected date range, go to Details > Forms and select Picking Slip from the Form drop-down list.

Allocate stock items

To allocate stock items to a service job:

  1. Access the desired job, then go to Stock > In Stock.
    • Alternatively, access the desired job, then go to Parts & Labour > Stock.
  2. Click the appropriate storage device.
  3. Locate and enter quantities for the desired items, then click Add Items.
  4. Click Billable to Manage parts and labour in a service job as normal.
  5. Click Save or Finish.
 Raise a purchase order within a service job

To raise a purchase order within a service job:

  1. Access the desired job, then go to Stock > Order.
  2. Under Purchase Type, select:
    • Blank to order an item not defined in the job.
    • Stock Still Required to raise a purchase order based on items in Stock > Required that have already been specified for the job from a quote.
    • Purchase Order Number to raise a blank purchase order to be updated with items prior to receipting the supplier invoice.
  3. In simPRO Enterprise, select a Purchasing Stage and Purchase Currency if applicable.
  4. Select whether you wish to base Purchase Prices on the supplier or job.
  5. Click Create Purchase Order.

Once you have created a Blank or Stock Still Required purchase order, you need to select the appropriate PO Type to order items:

From Catalogue

Select Pre-Build components or Catalogue items. You can then return to the Order to select a different Supplier, as well as edit Quantity and Pricing.

You can also click Show Cheapest Supplier / Show Cheapest Order if you have more than one supplier linked to an item.
From Description

Select to enter a Description and purchase Amount for the required item(s). As quantities and stock names cannot be specified for the system, you cannot partially receipt the order.

Leave the Inventory Item check box clear to ensure the order does not impact your stock levels in simPRO.
From Item Entry Select to order items not yet created or imported in the catalogue by manually entering the Part No, Stock Name, Quantity and Price. These would most likely be items you are unlikely to purchase again.

Once you have selected the PO Type, you need to select the Storage Device, enter Optional information, and enter Supplier Notes and Private Notes or select a script to specify delivery information. In simPRO Enterprise, you can also select a purchasing Stage.

When you have finished creating the purchase order, including adding or specifying the required items, click Finish > OK. Alternatively, click a purchase order in the confirmation box to exit the job and access the purchase order.

 Email a job form to the customer

Email job forms to the customer using the form templates assigned in System > Setup > Forms Setup > Job > Forms. You can also select and customise a Job Detail Form to send to the customer. If you need to email job cards to your employees, you can also do this here.

To email a job form to a customer:

  1. Access the desired job, then go to Details > Forms.
  2. Select the desired Form depending on the amount of detail you wish to provide the customer.
    • To send an email with no form, select Email Only.
  3. Click View to preview and / or print the form.
  4. Select a Primary Contact to address the form to and click to add additional Contacts if desired.
    • Alternatively, you can manually enter email addresses and separate them with a comma.
  5. Leave the Subject and Message fields blank to send the email template as defined in your forms setup.
    • Alternatively, enter a Subject and Message to override it.
  6. Click Send > OK to confirm.

A pop-up may then appear confirming the email address you have just sent the form to. Click OK to return to the job, then click Attach to attach a copy of the sent form to the job for your records.

Go to System > Setup > Forms Setup > Quote > Display Options > Form Title to enter a new form title to display on the printable form document. The form will still retain its simPRO default title when you select it from the Form drop-down list.

You can also enter a new Estimate Form Title to display when you select Show as Estimate when emailing a quote form.

Create a service job with multiple cost centres

You can create service jobs with multiple cost centres if the job requires works performed by different areas in your business. This process depends on whether you have single cost centre jobs enabled in System > Setup > Defaults > System > General > Service Jobs and Quotes.

To create a service job with multiple cost centres when you do not have single cost centre jobs enabled:

  1. Create the job as normal.
  2. Click Next.
  3. In Cost Centre List > Primary, select the desired Cost Centres from the drop-down box, then click Add.
  4. ​​Once you have built your job you may wish to use the Stage, Budget Status and Name / ID filter options to narrow your selection.
  5. Click Finish to create the job.

To create a service job with multiple cost centres when you do have single cost centre jobs enabled:

  1. Create a service job as normal.
  2. Click Next.
  3. Click Cost Centres in the breadcrumbs at the top of the job.
  4. In Cost Centre List > Primary, select the desired Cost Centres from the drop-down box, then click Add.
    • Once you have built your job you may wish to use the Stage, Budget Status and Name / ID filter options to narrow your selection.
  5. Click Finish to create the job.
 Create service job tasks

Create tasks within service jobs to keep an interactive to-do list for the job. Then when a task is due or has been updated, the assigned technician(s) as well as any other nominated stakeholders, such as customers or employers, will receive email notifications and simPRO alerts.

You can also add task templates created in your System Setup to add sets of tasks frequently carried out for certain types of jobs.

 Enable retention / retainage

Retention, also known as retainage in the United States, is the percentage of payment held by the customer to ensure the job is completed to specific standards and to safeguard against defects in workmanship.

You may wish to Enable retention / retainage for a service quote that you plan to merge into a project job. This is performed using the same process as for project quotes and is useful for calculating the amounts to be withheld to assist in invoicing.
