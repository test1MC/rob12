Use the QBO Accounting Link

Overview

It is recommended that you perform a regular export of your simPRO financial transactions to ensure your accounting package is up to date. Depending on your transaction load and operations, you may wish to perform this daily, multiple times a week, or weekly.

Go to Utilities > QuickBooks Online > Transfer to view, search and export simPRO transactions to QuickBooks Online.

Your transactions will be organised according to transaction type. Click Collapse All to collapse the transaction type tables and view only headings, the number of transactions per type, and the footer options. Click Expand All to view all transactions.

If a transaction has not yet been exported to QuickBooks Online, it will have a red N in the Exported column. If it has successfully posted to QuickBooks Online, it will have a green Y in the column. Transactions that have successfully exported will only appear if you search for them using the filter options.

You can filter your transactions by selecting from the following Search Types, then clicking Search:

Transaction Name / Number

Export simPRO transactions to QuickBooks Online

To export your transactions in simPRO to QuickBooks Online:

  1. Go to Utilities > QuickBooks Online > Transfer.
  2. Use the filter options to locate and select the transactions you wish to export.
  3. Alternatively, select Select All in the footer options to export all transactions.
  4. Click Send Selected > OK to confirm.

The Data Posting in Progress table will then appear, displaying your transactions being posted in real time. To cancel the export, click Stop Process.

When the export is complete, the Transaction History for Today table will appear where you can view the export Subject and Status of your transactions.

While the export is in progress, you can leave the export page if necessary and continue operating in other parts of simPRO. To return to view the export success, go to Utilities > QuickBooks Online > Connection.

 Mark transactions as exported in simPRO

You can manually mark transactions in simPRO to indicate that they have been exported from simPRO to the accounting package.

This is useful if the accounting link transfer was successful but the accounting package did not recognise that a transfer took place, so the applicable transactions were not marked as Exported in simPRO and still remain in the list to be transferred. You can then mark the applicable transactions as Exported to ensure you do not transfer duplicate transactions to your accounting package.

To mark transactions as exported in simPRO, select the applicable transactions, click Mark as exported in the footer options, then click OK to confirm.

Before marking transactions as exported, please ensure that you access QuickBooks Online to check the transactions have posted successfully.

Tax Invoice #

This will populate the Invoice # field in QuickBooks Online.

Date Issued

This will populate the Invoice date field in QuickBooks Online.

Payment Due

This will populate the Due date field in QuickBooks Online.

Payment Terms

This will populate the Terms field in QuickBooks Online if set up correctly.

Accounting category

This will populate the Location drop-down list in QuickBooks Online. Only applicable if you use accounting categories and have enabled location tracking in QuickBooks Online.

Imported invoices will then be broken down according to cost centre, then according to tax applied in simPRO. For example, if you are invoicing a service job containing two cost centres, and one of those cost centres has two different tax rates applied, this will break down in QuickBooks to appear as three separate line items.

Supplier invoices in QuickBooks Online will resemble an item breakdown, although the quantity will not be specified.

For Accounts Receivable / Debtors, this will be the expense account for the applicable cost centre. For Accounts Payable / Creditors, this will be the income account for the applicable cost centre.

Amount

Quantity

N/A

Class

The associated business group in simPRO. Only applicable if you use business groups and have enabled class tracking in QuickBooks Online.

If you export an invoice with a payment to QuickBooks Online with the correct invoice number but an incorrect customer name, the accounting link will reject it.

US only: You can select whether to include or exclude tax for each line item in your invoices in QuickBooks.

Use service items in QuickBooks Online

If you need to post sales invoices to different income accounts, for example, you have different income accounts set up for different cost centres, the QuickBooks Online accounting link will create service items for these cost centres to separate transactions appropriately. Subsequent invoices will then post to the correct income account in QuickBooks Online using these service items.

The accounting link will fail in the event that:

  • The name of the chart of account assigned to the service item is changed in QuickBooks Online. You will need to re-assign both the applicable cost centre in simPRO and the service item in QuickBooks Online to the correct income account.
  • The cost centre is assigned to a different income account in simPRO. You will then need to re-assign the applicable service item in QuickBooks Online to the correct income account.

If the name of a cost centre that is already linked to a service item is changed in simPRO, a new service item will be created with the new cost centre name.

To edit a service item in QuickBooks Online:

  1. Access your QuickBooks Online account as normal.
  2. Go to the company menu, then go to Lists > Products and Services.
  3. Click the applicable service item.
  4. In the Name field, enter the same name as the applicable cost centre exactly as it is formatted in simPRO in System > Setup > System Setup > Accounts > Cost Centres.
  5. You may wish to have simPRO open on your computer to ensure the names match exactly.
  6. Enter an SKU / Item/Service code if desired.
  7. Select the applicable Category if desired.
  8. Under Sales information, select I sell this product / service to my customers if applicable, then select the same Income account that is assigned to the applicable cost centre.
  9. Click Save and close.

For more information, please consult the QuickBooks Online help website.

Freight charges and restocking fees

For imported supplier invoices (also known as Bill Transactions in QBO), another line item will be added to include any applicable freight charges. Imported supplier credits (also known as Vendor Credit Transactions in QuickBooks Online) will include another line item for restocking fees if applicable.

To post freight charges or restocking fees to the correct accounts in QuickBooks Online, you need to set up the applicable Freight Account and / or Restocking Fee Account in simPRO in System > Setup > Defaults > Financial > Accounts.

UK only: CIS deductions

For imported customer invoices and contractor invoices, another line item will be added to include any CIS deductions.

To post CIS deductions to the correct accounts in QuickBooks Online, you need to set up the applicable CIS Tax Suffered Account (deductions on customer invoices) and CIS Liability Account (deductions on contractor invoices) in simPRO in System > Setup > Defaults > Financial > Accounts.

Please ensure you regularly reconcile your CIS amounts in QuickBooks Online using the CIS Monthly Returns Report.

Your customer and supplier payment terms will import from simPRO into QuickBooks Online in the following way:

Days from invoice date

This will appear as 'Net [specified number of days]' in the Terms field in QuickBooks Online. For example, if you have set a customer's payment terms to be seven days from the invoice date, this will appear as 'Net 7'.

Days after EOM

This will appear as 'EOM [specified number of days]' in the Terms field in QuickBooks Online. For example, if you have set a customer's payment terms to be seven days after the end of the month, this will appear as 'EOM 7'.

COD (cash on delivery)

This will appear as 'Due on receipt' in the Terms field in QuickBooks Online, and the 'Due in fixed number of days' option will be set to zero.

 Accounting categories and business groups

To track simPRO accounting categories (known as Locations in QuickBooks Online) or business groups (known as Classes in QuickBooks Online), you need to enable tracking in QuickBooks Online.

Accounting categories that have been applied to transactions such as invoices will populate the Location drop-down list in the top right corner of the transaction in QuickBooks Online.

Business groups will populate the Class area of individual line items within a transaction in QuickBooks Online.

You can only track either accounting categories or business groups; you cannot track both at once. If the accounting category (location) or business group (class) does not exist in QuickBooks Online, it will be created in the accounting package at the time that the transaction is being posted.
