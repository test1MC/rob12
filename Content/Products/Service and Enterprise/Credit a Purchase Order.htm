Credit a Purchase Order

Overview

Create a credit for a partially or fully receipted purchase order if, for example, there has been a shipping error or faulty goods supplied. You can create credits based on quantity, if you are returning goods, or based on price, if you are asking for a discount.

Create a credit

To credit a purchase order:

  1. Go to Materials > Purchase Orders > Receipts. Alternatively, open the desired purchase order and click the Receipts sub-tab.
  2. Click Options > Create Credit on the desired receipt.
  3. For the Credit Type, select:
    • Price for the supplier to give a full or partial discount for the goods supplied.
    • Quantity if you are returning the goods to the supplier.
    • You cannot change the credit type once the credit has been created.
  4. Depending on the Credit Type, enter the:
    • Credit Price: the cost discount on the item supplied. Alternatively, you can set the credit price to zero.
    • Credit Qty: the quantity of items being returned. Alternatively, you can set the credit quantity to zero.
  5. Enter the Freight amount, Restocking Fee and / or Tax Adjustment as applicable.
  6. Enter Supplier Notes and Private Notes as desired.
  7. Click Finish.

To send the credit to the supplier, click the Forms tab.

Enter the credit invoice number

Once the supplier has accepted the credit, add the Credit Invoice No. to the credit so that it will post as a transaction to your accounting link.

To add the Credit Invoice No:

  1. Go to Materials > Credits > Pending Credits.
  2. Click the desired credit, then click Unlock.
  3. Enter the Credit Invoice No.
  4. Enter the Credit Date.
  5. Click Finish > Finish.

The credit will then move to Complete Credits.

 Bulk set credit quantities / prices to zero

If you wish to only credit specific line items on a purchase order receipt, you can set all other line item quantities / prices to zero in bulk.

To bulk set credit quantities / prices to zero:

  1. Go to Materials > Purchase Orders.
  2. Create or access the desired credit as normal.
  3. Under Received Items, select the line items you do not wish to credit, then click Set Credit Qty to Zero / Set Credit Price to Zero.
    • Alternatively, select Select All, then click Set Qty to Zero to set all credit quantities / prices to zero.
  4. Continue crediting the purchase order receipt as normal.
 Email a supplier credit note form

You can email supplier credit notes to suppliers directly from simPRO.

To email a supplier credit note form:

  1. Go to Materials > Credits, then click the desired credit note.
    • Alternatively, create a supplier credit as normal, then click Finish.
  2. Click Forms.
  3. Under Form, ensure Supplier Credit is selected.
    • Alternatively, select Email Only to send an email with no form.
  4. Click View to preview and / or print the form.
  5. Select a Primary Contact to email the form to and click to add additional Contacts if desired.
    • Alternatively, you can manually enter email addresses and separate them with a comma.
  6. Adjust the default Subject and Body if desired.
    • Alternatively, click Clear Email to enter a new Subject and Body.
  7. Click Send > OK to confirm.

Go to System > Setup > Forms Setup > Purchase Order > Display Options > Form Titles to enter a new title to display on the printable form document. The form will still retain its simPRO default title when you select it from the Form drop-down list.
