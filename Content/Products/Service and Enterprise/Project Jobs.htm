Project Jobs

Overview

Create project jobs in simPRO to manage medium to large scale projects. You can monitor your costs, schedule technicians, create progress claims, oversee the project as it progresses, and make changes as you need to.

Recommended workflow

Click the steps below to view the corresponding instructions.

 Create a project job

You can create a project job outright in simPRO in the event that you have not converted it from a project quote.

To create a project job:

  1. Hover over Jobs, then click Create New Project Job.
  2. Select a Customer and Site.
    • Alternatively, click Create New to create a new customer or site.
  3. Select a Primary and Additional customer contact and a Site Contact.
  4. Enter the project Description to appear on the job card, in Connect, and on progress claims.
    • This will appear on job forms as the Overview Description.
  5. Enter an Order Number and / or Request Number if applicable.
  6. Select a Salesperson and Project Manager if applicable.
  7. Select Project Tags to categorise the job.
  8. Click Next.
Apply project-specific material and labour rates

Click Optional in the Job Setup page to adjust default job rates and settings such as pricing tiers, labour overheads, defaults, fit times and tax codes, material markups, discounts and fees, and item tax codes.

simPRO users in Australia can also adjust STC and VEEC values at the job setup stage.

 Add sections and cost centres

The main difference between a project job and a service job is that a project job is split into sections. You can create sections to define different stages of the job, such as Fit Off, Rough In and Commissioning, and add cost centres to each section. You can then add the applicable parts and labour to each cost centre.

To add sections:

  1. Access the desired project job, then click Cost Centre List.
  2. Enter the section Name.
  3. Enter a Description if desired.
    • This is for reference only and will not appear on any forms.
  4. Click Add.

Continue adding sections as desired. To add cost centres to sections, select the desired Cost Centres from the drop-down list, then click Add. You can then continue adding cost centres to each section as desired.

To move a cost centre from one section to another, click and drag the grey drag handle on the left of the cost centre.

You can also copy a cost centre to a different job or quote by clicking Options > Copy To, searching and selecting the desired job or quote, then clicking OK.

When you have set up all your sections and cost centres, start adding parts and labour.

 Add parts and labour

In a project job, parts and labour are added in each cost centre in each section. This is useful for budgeting how much you will delegate in each stage of the work.

To add parts and labour to a project job:

  1. Access the desired project job, then click Cost Centre List.
  2. Locate the desired section, then click the applicable cost centre.
  3. Click Parts and Labour.
  4. Search and add pre-builds and catalogue items as desired.
  5. Click Billable.
  6. Select one or more labour rates as desired, then click Add.
  7. Adjust the estimated Time as applicable.
    • When the labour rate check box is selected, the time is automatically calculated from the added materials in the Parts table. If you enter a new Time, the labour rate check box will clear and Billable Time will no longer be based on Estimated Time.
  8. Click Save.
  9. To return to the Cost Centre List, click Cost Centres in the breadcrumbs at the top of the project job.

You can then continue adding parts and labour for each cost centre in each section. Once you have added parts to the job, you will need to allocate stock from your storage devices so that your stock levels are accurate.

You can also add take off templates to a project job. Keep in mind that you can only add a take off template to one section, so if the take off template encompasses a large amount of work, it may be best to add the individual components to each section where they are needed.

 Manage stock in a project job

Go to Stock > All in a project job cost centre to manage all required or allocated stock items. You can filter stock items according to Storage Location (storage device), or alternatively, perform a local search by entering the part number, description or search terms, then clicking Search.

In Stock > Required, you can manage required items that have not yet been ordered or allocated from stock. These are items that have been added in Parts & Labour. In Stock > Allocated, manage items that have been assigned to the job. This is particularly useful for adjusting overallocation of stock.

Click the black arrows or enter quantities in the Assigned column to adjust how many items have been assigned to the cost centre. You cannot assign more items than are Required. When you have finished making changes, click Update Stock > Save. Then, go to Billable > Parts to ensure your changes have been reflected.

To allocate stock items to a project job:

  1. Access the desired project job, then click Cost Centre List.
  2. Locate the desired section, then click the applicable cost centre.
  3. Go to Stock > In Stock.
    • Alternatively, access the desired job, then go to Parts & Labour > Stock.
  4. Click the applicable storage device.
  5. Locate and enter quantities for the desired items, then click Add Items.
  6. Click Save or Finish.

If you do not have the necessary items in stock, create a purchase order for materials still required to order items directly to the job.

To view, print, email or attach a list of all stock items assigned to a project job during a selected date range, go to Details > Forms and select Picking Slip from the Form drop-down list.

 Add customer assets

If the project involves servicing customer assets on site, you can add and manage them in Customer Assets in the relevant cost centre.

To add a customer asset to the project job:

  1. Access the desired project job, then click Cost Centre List.
  2. Locate the desired section, then click the applicable cost centre.
  3. Click Customer Assets.
  4. Under Add Asset, search and select the desired asset.
  5. Click Save.
 Enable retention / retainage

Retention, also known as retainage in the United States, is the percentage of payment held by the customer to ensure the job is completed to specific standards and to safeguard against defects in workmanship.

If retention / retainage was not applied to the quote before conversion, you can enable it in the job. simPRO will assist in tracking the retention held back from your customer, as well as in claiming your retention back on your final invoice and your period rebate claim with a reminder to invoice once the work has been signed off on.

To enable retention / retainage for a project job:

  1. Access the desired project job, then go to Details > Settings > Retention / Retainage.
  2. Click Enable Retention / Retainage.
  3. Enter the retention / retainage Period and Retention Amount percentage.
    • You can also enter the Amount Per Claim percentage if desired.
  4. Enter the Final Claim Rebate percentage.
  5. Click Save or Finish.
 Order materials

Order materials for a section of the project before starting work. You can create purchase orders for an entire section or for each cost centre.

To order materials for a particular stage:

  1. Access the desired project job, then go to Project Overview > Materials.
  2. Select the check box for the desired section.
  3. In the footer options, select the Purchase Stage if desired.
  4. Select whether Purchase Prices are Based on Supplier or Based on Job.
  5. Select the Purchase Currency if applicable.
    • Otherwise, select Auto Select.
  6. Click Create Purchase Order.
    • If you are creating a supplier quote, click Create Supplier Quote.

The purchase order will have all of the catalogue items added to each cost centre in the section. You can then send the purchase order as normal.

 Schedule the project

For a project job, it is often easiest to schedule technicians using Day View or Week View. To schedule using Day View or Week View, the job cannot be currently open.

To schedule a project job for several employees over several days:

  1. Go to Schedules > Week View / Day View.
  2. Select Job from the drop-down list next to the search bar, then search and select the desired project job.
  3. Select the desired cost centre next to the job number.
  4. Select the desired date / week at the top of the schedule.
  5. Under Employee, select All Available Employees.
    • In Day View, you may need to select Show All to see all employees.
  6. Click and drag on the desired day to create a schedule block.
    • Adjust the length of individual schedules by clicking and dragging the bottom of the schedule block.
  7. To copy the same schedule to other employees or days:
    • Right-click the schedule block and click Copy Schedule, then right-click on another employee or another day and click Paste Schedule.
  8. Once all applicable employees are scheduled, click Finish.

You can also schedule one technician at a time using the Schedule tab within each cost centre in the job. See Schedule a service job from the Schedule tab in our Service Jobs article for more information.

 Raise contractor work orders

Raise contractor work orders based on an hourly or set rate. Contractors with an hourly rate set in their card files can be scheduled to jobs the same way as employees, which will automatically create the work order.

You can also schedule contractors in the schedule Day View / Week View, and email the work order to the contractor.

Create work orders based on an hourly rate

Schedule a contractor with an hourly rate to automatically create a work order.

To schedule a contractor:

  1. Access the desired project job, then click Cost Centre List.
  2. Locate the desired section, then click the applicable cost centre.
  3. Click Schedule.
  4. Click the schedule icon next to the contractor you wish to schedule.
  5. Click and drag the schedule block to the desired day and time.
    • To extend or shorten a schedule block, hover over the top or bottom edge, then click and drag the white arrows.
  6. Click the close icon in the top right corner.
  7. Click Save or Finish.

The labour cost of the work order will automatically be calculated based on the contractor's pay rate and scheduled hours.

 Create work orders based on a set price

To create a work order for a contractor with a set price, you can enter the set price either as the cost of labour or cost of materials on the work order.

To create a set price work order:

  1. Access the desired project job, then click Cost Centre List.
  2. Locate the desired section, then click the applicable cost centre.
  3. Go to Details > Contractor.
  4. Click New Work Order.
  5. Select the Contractor and Employee.
  6. Select the Date.
  7. Select Applying Retention? if applicable, then enter the Retention Amount, Amount Per Claim, and Retention Period.
  8. Enter a Description.
    • Alternatively, select a script.
  9. Clear the Schedule Based check box and enter the set price value as either a Labour or Materials cost as desired.
  10. Select the applicable Tax Code.
  11. Click Finish.
 Add variations

Use standard or linked variations to handle extra works that fall outside of the scope of estimation or add-ons as requested by the customer.

A standard variation is a separate cost centre within the same job and is invoiced with the job, while a linked variation is an entirely separate job / quote and is invoiced separately from the original job.

Add a standard variation

To create a standard variation:

  1. Access the desired project job, then go to Cost Centre List > Variations.
  2. Enter a descriptive variation section Name, then click Add.
  3. Select Include section value in retention / retainage totals if desired.
  4. Select the desired cost centre(s) from the drop-down list, then click Add.
  5. Click a cost centre to Add parts and labour to the variation as normal.
  6. Click Save or Finish to update your changes.

As the cost centre will not be locked, standard variations are generally ‘do and charge’ with labour and materials to be invoiced.

If you wish to perform this variation for a fixed price, add your parts and labour first, then lock the cost centre. Alternatively, use a linked quote variation.

 Add a linked variation

The Linked Variations sub-tab will appear after you have added parts or labour in a cost centre.

To create a linked variation:

  1. Access the desired project job, then go to Cost Centre List > Linked Variations.
  2. Click Create Variation under Quotes / Jobs.
  3. Click OK to confirm.
    • This will create a new job or quote for the same customer and site.
  4. In the newly created quote / job, click Next.
  5. Create the variation as normal and Add sections and cost centres and Add parts and labour.
  6. Click Save or Finish to update your changes.

The variation will appear under Pending Quotes / Jobs with the original job number as well as the variation number. If you have the original job and the linked variation open at the same time, you will need to click Finish > Edit Job in the original job to update the Linked Variation sub-tab.

If you have created a linked quote variation that has then been converted to a job, your customer may wish to see this variation on the same invoice as the original project. You can merge your linked job variation with the project which will add an extra section under the Primary tab.

 Raise progress claims

Raise progress claims to invoice each stage of the job as they are completed. Once the final stage is complete, you can start the final progress claim.

 Manage a project from the Project Overview

When the job is underway, use the Project Overview to keep track of your material and labour costs against your estimated budget and make forecasted changes to your current budget to account for any unforeseen challenges that may arise.

You can also access any invoices, purchase orders and work orders associated with the project, set milestones as parts of your project are completed, and review metrics of the project in the chart such as invoiced and invoiced profit against actual material and labour costs.

If your project was not converted from a quote, you can edit cost centre totals, lock individual cost centres and lock the whole project job. For projects that have been converted from quotes, the values are locked and making changes from the Project Overview will not update the project.

View projected costs

Go to Project Overview > Projected to view the cost to complete the project.

Select Operations View to view both the grand total as well as the original budget, current budget, and actual budget. You can also select to have Actuals Include Overhead, have Actuals Include Committed Cost, as well as Show Variations and Show Revised Budget. This view also includes a chart as a visual representation of your budgets.

Select Financial View to view only the current budget and actual margin. You can select to have Actuals Include Overhead and Actuals Include Committed Cost.

 Manage project materials

Go to Project Overview > Materials to track all materials as they are used on the project. You may also adjust the Projected Remaining Cost of your materials budget, which will then adjust your current budget.

To adjust the sell price or markup for all cost centres, enter the adjustment in the Adjust Material Sell / Adjust Material Markup field near the top of the page and click Update > Save.

 Manage project resources

Go to Project Overview > Resources to adjust your Projected Remaining Hours by Cost or Hours. This will then adjust your current budget.

To adjust the labour hours or labour markup for an individual cost centre, enter the adjustment in the correct column and click Save. To adjust the labour hours or labour markup for all cost centres, enter the adjustment in the Global Adjust Hours / Will Adjust Hours by % / Adjust Labour Markup field near the top of the page, then click Update > Save.

To download a CSV file of the job resources, click the download icon in the top right.

 Adjust the project budget

Go to Project Overview > Adjustments to view a summary and chart of the project budget as well as make any of the same adjustments made in Materials and Resources. You can also add a fee or discount to the job.

To apply a fee or discount:

  1. Under Adjustment type, select Down (Discount) / Up (Fee).
  2. Enter the adjustment as a Value or Percentage.
  3. Click Update Quote > Save.

The fee / discount will display as a separate colour in the chart on the right.

Click Lock Job to lock the job, as well as either estimates or items according to your settings in System > Setup > Defaults > System.

To print or download the chart, click the list icon in the top right of the chart.

 Monitor job production

Go to Project Overview > Production to revise work completed on the job to date. As allocated materials are used on the project, select the Date of revision, enter the quantity in the Completed column, then click Save Revision. This helps you keep track of what materials are being used on specific days throughout the project's duration. You can also view previous revisions by selecting them from the Revision drop-down list.

Below the list of materials, view the totals of the revision and a graph of the labour cost, material cost, total cost, invoiced, and invoiced profit over the course of the project. To print or download the graph, click the list icon.

To delete a revision, select it from the drop-down list and click Delete Revision.

 View contractor work orders

You can view a list of work orders raised for the job in Project Overview > Contractor Jobs.

 View purchase orders

Go to Project Overview > Orders to view a list of all purchase orders raised for the project job to Order materials.

 Raise the final progress claim

Raise the final progress claim the same way as previous claims, but make sure you click Finalise Claim. After the final claim, the job will be moved to Jobs > Invoiced and you cannot raise any more progress claims.
