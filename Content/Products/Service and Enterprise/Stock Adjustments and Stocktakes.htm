Stock Adjustments and Stocktakes

Overview

Stock adjustments allow you to periodically correct stock levels without putting a burden on resources to perform a full stocktake. This is useful for technicians allocating stock in the field using Connect, as well as for improving your stock value reporting. For example, if your stock count accuracy is being negatively impacted by staff not following the correct stock management process, you can perform a stock adjustment on one or several items found to be incorrect, take note of negative or positive variations, and educate the applicable staff members about following the process correctly.

Performing a stocktake at least once a year, or more frequently depending on the size of your inventory, is an important part of maintaining accurate stock reporting. Compared to stock adjustments, which are meant for corrections, a stocktake should be an end-to-end process and a total count of all physical stock in a storage device (location). This should be done prior to financial stock value reporting where practical.

When performing stock adjustments or stocktakes, it is important that your catalogue pricing is up to date in order to accurately cost materials onto jobs. As there is no impact on inventory or accounts until a stocktake or adjustment has been finished and approved, it is also recommended to lock down the storage device that is being counted to prevent any transactions of inventory.

View stock adjustments and stocktakes

To view your stock adjustments and stocktakes, hover over Materials in the main menu, then click Stock Takes.

To search for a stock adjustment or stocktake, enter the Stock Take No., Storage Device or the employee name that created it in the local search bar, then click Search. Alternatively, click Advanced to filter search results according to who they were Created By or Approved By, the Storage Location (storage device), or the Stock Take Stage.

Click Options on a stock adjustment or stocktake to open it or delete it. If it has been approved, you can also click Copy Stock Take to create an identical stock adjustment or stocktake for another storage device.

 Perform a stock adjustment

To perform a stock adjustment:

  1. Go to Materials > Stock, then click Options > Stock Take on the desired storage device.
    • Alternatively, go to Materials > Stock Take > Create Stock Take, then select the desired storage device and click Create.
  2. Confirm the Type is a Stock Adjustment.
  3. Adjust the Counted Stock quantity of each item to reflect that of your physical stock.
    • Alternatively, enter a Variation quantity, that is, the difference between your Counted Stock and System Stock (stock levels currently recorded in simPRO).
  4. To add stock items not currently in the storage device in simPRO, click Catalogue.
  5. Locate the required items manually or using the local search bar, then enter quantities and click Add Items.
    • You may wish to use a barcode scanner to make this process more efficient if you have imported barcodes as search terms against your catalogue items.
  6. Click Save.
  7. Verify your stock counts and total cost pricing and make any changes, then click Finish & Approve.
    • ​​Alternatively, click Finish if you wish to save the stock adjustment as Pending to be updated and approved later.

 Perform a stocktake

To perform a stocktake:

  1. Go to Materials > Stock, then click Options > Stock Take on the desired storage device.
    • Alternatively, go to Materials > Stock Take > Create Stock Take, then select the desired storage device and click Create.
  2. Select Stock Take from the Type drop-down list.
  3. Click Forms.
  4. Select the desired stocktake form from the Form drop-down list, then click View to preview and / or print the form.
    • Alternatively, enter an email address, Subject and Message, then click Send to email the stocktake form.
  5. Click Change Type.
    • This will reset any figures entered in the Variation column to zero.
  6. Click Catalogue.
  7. Locate the required items manually or using the local search bar, then enter quantities and click Add Items.
    • You may wish to use a barcode scanner to make this process more efficient if you have imported barcodes as search terms against your catalogue items.
  8. Click Stock Take.
  9. Verify your Counted Stock / Variation figures as required.
    • You may also wish to ensure your catalogue default supplier pricing against each part is accurate, as this will be the assumed cost of each item in your stocktake.
  10. Click Save > Finish & Approve.
    • Alternatively, click Finish if you wish to save the stocktake as Pending to be updated and approved later.
 Approve pending stock adjustments and stocktakes

To approve a pending stock adjustment or stocktake, go to Materials > Stock Takes, then use the Approved column or the Advanced search options.

Click the stock adjustment or stocktake, verify the Counted Stock / Variation figures, then click Finish & Approve.

 Print a stocktake form

You can print stocktake forms as an additional reference when performing a stock adjustment or stocktake.

To print a stocktake form:

  1. Create a stock adjustment / stocktake as normal.
  2. Click Forms.
  3. Select the desired stocktake form from the Form drop-down list, then click View to preview and / or print the form.
    • Alternatively, enter an email address, Subject and Message, then click Send to email the stocktake form.
  4. Click Stock Take to continue performing the stock adjustment / stocktake.
