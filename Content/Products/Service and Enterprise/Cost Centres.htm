Cost Centres

Overview

Use cost centres to separate operational costs within your business. Cost centres are an integral part of simPRO and are involved in the job and costing process. Cost centres and their names can reflect different types of work to assist in reporting. Cost centres can be grouped together in business groups.

Create a cost centre

To create a cost centre:

  1. Go to System > Setup > Accounts > Cost Centres.
  2. Click Create Cost Centre.
  3. Enter the Cost Centre Name.
  4. Enter the Income Account No. and Expense Account No. These refer to the default accounts set up in System > Setup > Defaults > Financial.
  5. Enter the Monthly Sales Budget and Monthly Expenditure Budget.
  6. Click Create.
 Assign cost centres to an employee

Employees can only be scheduled to jobs if they are assigned to the job's cost centre.

To assign cost centres to an employee:

  1. Go to People > Employees.
  2. Click the desired employee.
  3. Click the Schedule sub-tab under the Profile tab.
  4. Under Cost Centres, click Available Cost Centres to assign them. Click Assigned Cost Centres to remove them from the employee. Alternatively, you can Add All or Remove All.

Assign the appropriate cost centres to all of your employees based on the work they will be doing.

 Assign cost centres to leads, quotes and jobs

When you create a new quote or job, you will need to select a cost centre. In Enterprise, quotes and jobs can have multiple cost centres, and parts and labour are added and schedules are created within each cost centre. This means that you can have two (or more) technicians performing different kinds of work at the same job site and you can easily keep track of their separate costs.

You can also assign cost centres to Leads.
